## Key Finding: Nicky Gibson — CONFIRMED POKE CREDIT

### Source: nickygibson.com/about
**URL:** https://www.nickygibson.com/about

Nicky Gibson's official portfolio site explicitly lists both **Kate Moss** and **Topshop** as clients under the POKE section of her career:

> _Clients / Poke_
> **BBC — Kate Moss — Orange (Unlimited / Balloonacy) — Skype — Good Food — Topshop — Diesel — French Connection — Global Rich List — Baker Tweet — Gardener's World — Mother**

This is the strongest individual credit found to date. Nicky Gibson:
- Was Senior Art Director / Designer at POKE London
- Lists Kate Moss and Topshop separately AND together as her POKE-era clients
- Is described as "multi-award winning — MoMa and Design Museum exhibited — digital design director, creative & artist"
- Has 22+ years of experience
- Describes herself as "Previously at Poke, Deepend, Razorfish, and Lost My Name"

### Source: creativebloq.com/3d/iain-tait-7099043
**URL:** https://www.creativebloq.com/3d/iain-tait-7099043
**Published:** July 2009

A Creative Bloq interview with Iain Tait about POKE London. This article confirms Nicky Gibson's title at the time of the Balloonacy project (which was closely concurrent with the Kate Moss work):

> _"Design-wise, too, the concept threw up some hurdles. 'This was an event with a defined time frame, so it was quite a unique challenge in terms of visual communication,' suggests **senior art director Nicky Gibson**."_

This confirms her title: **Senior Art Director** at POKE London.

The same article names other key POKE staff of the era:
- **Iain Tait** — Co-Founder (interviewed throughout)
- **Nik Roope** — "BakerTweet was the brainchild of Nik Roope, Poke's co-founder and co-creative director, and a former colleague of Tait's from the aptly named Oven Digital."
- **Derek McKenna** — Lead Flash Developer ("handled the client-side gameplay and the bulk of the user interface")
- **Gabriel Bucknall** — Developer ("created the balloons and 3D race map")
- **Caroline Butterworth** — Developer/animator ("embeddable widgets and character animations")
- **Andrew Zolty** — Developer (BakerTweet Arduino)
- **Jason Fox** — Senior Creative (Oasis/Rubberduckzilla AR project)

**Note:** These credits are for the Balloonacy (Orange) and BakerTweet projects, not explicitly Kate Moss/Topshop. However, as contemporaneous POKE staff (2007–2009), they are part of the studio team working in the same period.

## Award Entries: Topshop-Specific Attribution Status

### Webby Awards
- Nicky Gibson's awards page lists two Webby wins (Design Aesthetic; Telecommunications).
- The Telecommunications Webby is consistent with Orange work (Balloonacy/Unlimited), not Topshop.
- "Design Aesthetic" is a category that could apply to the Topshop microsite.
- **No direct confirmation** that either Webby on Gibson's page was for Kate Moss / Topshop.

### BIMA
- Gibson lists BIMA Overall Winner — BTC Web; Winner — BTC & Websites; Winner — Technology & Innovation.
- The Topshop microsite launched November 2006 per the WebWire press release (Topshop.com relaunched Nov 2006), meaning it could have been submitted for BIMA 2007 awards.
- Campaign Live (article 770872) notes "Topshop recently unveiled a branded microsite supporting the launch of its exclusive clothing range designed by supermodel Kate Moss" — confirming the microsite existed.
- **No direct confirmation** of a BIMA award specifically for Kate Moss / Topshop.

### Revolution Awards
- Gibson lists Revolution Awards Grand Prix and Best Online Advertising.
- The Revolution Awards Grand Prix in 2007/08 era would be a significant recognition.
- **Cannot confirm** which project won without primary award source data.

### One Show Interactive
- Gibson lists Silver Pencil — Consumer Microsites.
- "Consumer Microsites" is the most likely category for the Kate Moss / Topshop microsite.
- **Cannot confirm** attribution to this project without primary award entry data.
