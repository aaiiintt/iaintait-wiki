### W+K London — Official Case Study Page
- **URL:** https://wklondon.com/work/food-dancing/
- **Published:** 2017
- **Tier:** 1
- **Relevance:** credits, video
- **Key quotes / facts:** "The initiative aimed to 'drive brand warmth and mental availability for Sainsbury's by re-energising the brand with a fresh, distinctive take on food.' The creative centred on celebrating spontaneous kitchen dancing—'that personal moment when you're in the kitchen, you're totally in the rhythm of cooking.'" Confirms: song "Food Dancing (Yum Yum Yum)" by MysDiggi; Director Siri Bunford (Knucklehead); campaign reinforced Sainsbury's endline "live well for less"; metrics confirmed (85.6M social impressions, 96% positive/neutral Twitter sentiment, 16.8M GIF views, 39M TV reach, #1 UK Spotify Viral Chart 10 Feb 2017); awards: Gold and three silver at FAB Awards. Named Sainsbury's colleague Richard Hetherson (body pop/locking baker) as a notable participant.

### W+K London — Blog Post (Campaign Announcement)
- **URL:** https://wklondon.com/2017/01/fooddancing-wiedenkennedys-first-campaign-sainsburys/
- **Published:** 18 January 2017
- **Tier:** 1
- **Relevance:** credits, press
- **Key quotes / facts:** Confirms launch date January 2017. Campaign positioned as debut W+K work for Sainsbury's. Details: "Food lies at the heart of living well; bringing us joy and making us feel good." Through-the-line execution with online reward incentives for public participation. Music: original song commissioned from MysDiggi (described as "former Sainsbury's employee" / "UK artist with personal Sainsbury's connection"). Campaign includes TV, music video, in-store/OOH posters, digital rewards layer. Confirmed food photographer David Sykes (colourful food shots) and portrait photographer Josh Cole (black-and-white) as distinct visual voices in the campaign.
