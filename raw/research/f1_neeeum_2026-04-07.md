## 2. What is NEEEUM?

NEEEUM is a **multi-component campaign** with the following distinct elements:

1. **The sonic identity / sound logo:** "WGTT15000BPM F1 NEEEUM MIX" — The Chemical Brothers' track "We've Got To Try" accelerated to 15,000 BPM to match F1's 15,000 RPM engine rev limit. Result: 3 seconds of sound. This became the official **new global sonic identity for F1**.
2. **Series of 6-second films:** Accompanying social content showing a dog (Girl the Dog) driving an F1 car and commandeering a rocket into space. Released alongside the sonic identity on 7 March 2019.
3. **Full music video for "We've Got To Try":** Released 8 March 2019. Directed by Ninian Doff, produced by Pulse Films. Tells the story of "Girl the Dog" completing the impossible.
4. **Season launch film:** Debuted 11 March 2019, using the full Chemical Brothers track to hype fans for the first 2019 Grand Prix in Melbourne.

The campaign launched the 2019 F1 season — the 1,000th race season.

## 4. The Chemical Brothers Connection — Commission or Pre-Existing Track?

The Drum article on Globe/Universal Music provides the definitive backstory:

> "Most recently, to help Formula One celebrate its 1,000th race, Globe delivered a partnership with The Chemical Brothers. A single, an audio brand and a music video were just some of the executions born from **what was started as a conversation about licensing a song.**"

So: **it began as a licensing conversation** — W+K/F1 wanted to use a Chemical Brothers track; Globe (Universal Music's creative agency) facilitated this. Through the process it evolved into a far larger collaboration: a bespoke remix, a full sonic identity, an F1-inspired music video, and a season launch film.

The Chemical Brothers were already fans of F1 (long-standing; had performed at multiple F1 events). The track "We've Got To Try" was from their forthcoming album *No Geography* — it was a pre-existing new track, not composed for F1. But the remix was a genuine engineering exercise commissioned for the partnership.

Globe also helped bankroll/support the music video for the track — a mutual benefit arrangement.

The album *No Geography* reached No. 2 in the UK charts (partly boosted by the F1 collaboration publicity).

## 5. The W+K London / F1 Relationship

This is part of an ongoing and deep W+K London relationship with Formula 1, which spans at least four major campaigns:

1. **Formula 1 Rebrand / "Unleashed"** — November 2017 — W+K London redesigned the F1 visual identity (first time in 23 years), logo, typography, brand behaviours. Led by W+K design lead Richard Turley. Covered by 1,500+ media outlets, 35,000+ tweets. Creative Review exclusive was the title's most-read article of 2017.
   - URL: https://wklondon.com/work/formula-1/
   - URL: https://www.wk.com/work/formula-1-rebrand/

2. **"Engineered Insanity"** — March 2018 — F1's first ever global marketing campaign. Results: 10% increase in unique viewers (reaching 490.2m), 53% YoY growth in social followers to 18.5m, 4m+ Grand Prix attendees (+2.7%).
   - URL: https://wklondon.com/work/engineered-insanity/

3. **NEEEUM** — March 2019 — This campaign (sonic identity + music video + season launch film).
   - URL: https://wklondon.com/work/neeeum/

4. **"Still Rising"** — 2020 — Celebrating Lewis Hamilton's 7th World Title.
   - URL: https://wklondon.com/work/still-rising/

5. **"One Begins"** — 2021 — Designed livery and launch film for the new 2022 F1 car.
   - URL: https://wklondon.com/work/one-begins/

Context: F1 was purchased by Liberty Media in 2017. New CMO Ellie Norman came in with a mandate to build F1 as a global media and entertainment brand (not just a B2B sponsorship vehicle). W+K London was the agency that architected this entire brand transformation.
