- **Winner: Orange 'Balloonacy' — POKE**

## Dimension 4: Metrics (verify and expand)

### Participants / Pilots
- **40,000+ pilots** (balloons entered) — confirmed by Orange's own 2010 press release and awards.playballoonacy.com Wayback capture (which says "42,000 Balloonists")
- Note: Existing file says "30,000 balloons." The primary sources say 40,000-42,000. This likely reflects the final settled figure; 30,000 may have been an in-race count or early estimate.

### Race Course Sites
- **1,500+ websites** — confirmed by 2010 Orange press release ("over 1,500 selected websites")
- Wayback capture of awards.playballoonacy.com says "2,000 Race Host Sites" — suggesting the final count was higher than sometimes reported

### Distance / Miles
- **63 million online miles** total raced — confirmed by 2010 Orange press release
- Existing file says winning balloon "Reginald Ringtail the Raccoon" covered 5,033 internet miles — this is a specific claim not independently verified in this pass but plausible as a single race winner figure

### Duration
- Race ran for 7 days (stated on crackunit.com announcement post)
- Launch: 24 June 2008 (confirmed by crackunit.com launch post)
- Announcement/sign-up: 2 June 2008 (3 weeks before launch, per crackunit.com post)

### Prize
- Grand prize: £20,000 VIP trip to Ibiza for winner and 7 friends — confirmed by crackunit.com announcement post

### Scale context
- Described by one commenter on crackunit.com as "one of the big online ideas of 2008"
- Campaign Live called it "one of the biggest online campaigns of 2008"
- Orange's own press release: "Top 10 most awarded interactive projects of 2009" globally

### Widget Platform
- Built on Gigya widget platform (confirmed by crackunit.com "More Balloonacy" post, 6 June 2008)
- 3D map built in Papervision (confirmed by awards.playballoonacy.com Wayback archive)

## Dimension 5: Iain's Role (confirmed as CD/ECD and originator)

- **Confirmed as originator of the idea and Creative Director / ECD at POKE** — per Iain Tait's own statement (provided as context for this research)
- crackunit.com is confirmed as Iain's personal blog. His bio on the site: "Ex-ECD at W+K London. Ex-Google Creative Lab NYC. Ex-Poke Founder."
- His announcement post (2 June 2008) uses first-person: "Here's the start of another major thing that we've been working on for Orange for a while now…"
- His launch post (24 June 2008) uses first-person: "After lots of late nights and hard work by lots of valiant people at Poke…"
- Iain entered the race himself with a camel-shaped balloon (URL above)
- The project was **purely in-house at POKE** — no external production company or director
- **i-level was the media partner** (not a creative partner). Note: i-level appears in the Megas 2009 winners list for a *different* project (HPV Vaccine with COI/Department of Health), confirming they were active in this period and in the same award show — but as a separate entity from Poke's work on Balloonacy.

## Dimension 6: Cultural Legacy

- Referenced in Campaign Live as a case study for branded online games as recently as November 2009
- Prompted a direct sequel campaign in 2010 (run by Martin Rose, not Iain — confirmed by Iain)
- 2010 press release from Orange/Everything Everywhere explicitly invokes 2008 as the benchmark: "aims to be even more successful than the 2008 Balloonacy campaign"
- Cited as evidence of digital-only campaigns' growing power in Campaign's 2008 year-end digital round-up
- Wayback Machine has 34 captures of the awards site, indicating sustained industry interest
- One of the Top 10 most awarded interactive projects of 2009 globally (per Orange's own claim)
- The crackunit.com announcement post received a comment calling it "the best thing [Poke had] done yet for Orange" and "beats uniqlock hands down" — uniqlock was a major Cannes Grand Prix winner at the time, indicating how highly the industry rated the idea even pre-launch

## Summary

### What is VERIFIED
- Campaign launched 24 June 2008 by POKE London for Orange UK
- Iain Tait was Creative Director/ECD and originator of the idea
- Entirely in-house build at POKE; i-level was media partner only
- Scale: 40,000–42,000 pilots, 1,500–2,000 race host sites, 63 million online miles, 7-day race
- Prize: £20,000 Ibiza trip
- Widget platform: Gigya. 3D map: Papervision
- **MediaGuardian Innovation Award 2009 — Digital Creative category — WIN** (Guardian, 25–30 Mar 2009)
- **Webby Award 2009 — Websites / Telecommunications category — WIN** (winners.webbyawards.com/2009/websites/general-websites/telecommunications/152435/orange-balloonacy)
- Nominated (not confirmed winner) in Webby Interactive Advertising - Game or Application category
- Nine awards total from 2008 campaign (per Orange's own 2010 press release)
- "Top 10 most awarded interactive projects of 2009" globally (per Orange's own claim)
- Three live crackunit.com posts by Iain Tait from June 2008
- Webby archived awards page live at Wayback Machine
- YouTube video exists: `https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wd3-Va5ePwk` (Contagious/POKE interview, needs watching to confirm 2008 vs 2010)
- Campaign credited in trade press as "one of the biggest online campaigns of 2008"
