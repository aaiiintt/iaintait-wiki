**Date:** 2026-04-06

**Agency:** POKE London

## Dimension 1: Work Online

### What the campaign was
- A GPS tracker was attached to a real bull in a real field at a secret UK location (not at Glastonbury)
- The bull's position was live-streamed to a dedicated microsite at **spotthebull.co.uk** (also referenced as **spotthebull.orange.co.uk**)
- The field was divided into a grid of squares
- Daily competition mechanic: pick the grid square where you thought the bull would be standing at 3pm each day
- Correct guesses entered a prize draw to win a pair of Glastonbury Festival tickets (sold out, making them extremely high-value)
- The bull in the 2009 iteration was named **Desmond** (the Creative Bloq/Iain Tait interview refers to an earlier bull named **Derek**)
- In 2008, the relaunch added the ability to track the bull's "hoof steps" and view his highlights from the day; a farmer also gave tips on the bull's daily routine

### Technology (2009 iteration especially)
- **Papervision3D** (Flash AS3) for 3D rendering
- Live two-way data feed
- GPS tracking of the bull relayed back into the ad unit
- 3D banner ad format developed via **Flashtalking** (showcase at flashtalking.com/showcase/Orange3D/)
- Production partners: **POKE** (creative/digital) and **Unit 9** (production)
- Media planning and buying: **Mediaedge CIA**

### Campaign timeline (confirmed iterations)
- **2007** — Original campaign launched. Named #1 in Campaign Annual's Top 10 Digital Ads of 2007: "A brilliantly funny idea that really took off. Orange had some Glastonbury tickets to give away, so Poke put a GPS tracker on a bull..." — TrendHunter covered it in May 2007.
- **2008** — Relaunched May 2008. Campaign Live headline: "Orange revives Spot the Bull" (30 May 2008) and "Orange launches Glastonbury ticket giveaway online" (27 May 2008). Bull could now be tracked hoof-step by hoof-step with daily highlights.
- **2009** — Ran again. Bull named Desmond, followed on Twitter at @spotthebull. AdPulp covered it 27 May 2009: "Ride Darling Desmond To The Show."

## Dimension 6: Cultural Legacy (physical-digital integration pioneer)

### Why this matters historically

Spot the Bull is one of the earliest documented examples of:
1. **Physical-digital real-time integration in UK advertising** — a real animal in a real field, GPS-tracked, with data streamed into an interactive digital product
2. **IoT-before-IoT** — connecting a physical object (a bull) to the internet to drive digital engagement, predating the mainstream IoT discourse
3. **Gamification of a live physical event** — users competing based on real-world unpredictable behaviour
4. **Contextual prize mechanics** — the prize (Glastonbury tickets) was organically connected to Orange's brand (Orange was the principal sponsor of Glastonbury at the time)

### Documented legacy

**Walkers' "When it Rains" (cited explicitly by Mando Promotions):**
> "It was this promotion which inspired Walkers' When it Rains promotion which was a much bigger promotional campaign involving consumers purchasing crisps and picking grid squares across the country to participate — by guessing where it was going to rain."

The Mando article also notes: "Since that, beer brands in particular have chosen to run weather-related sales promotions to capture consumer's attention."

**Poke 10th birthday (2011):** When POKE celebrated their 10th anniversary, Spot the Bull was chosen as one of the representative works to be commemorated with a bespoke cake table — indicating it was considered one of the agency's landmark pieces.

**Adsspot.me catalogue:** Lists it among POKE London's significant work alongside Orange Unlimited, Orange Balloonacy, and others.

## Summary

**Spot the Bull** is a campaign that ran for at least three consecutive years (2007, 2008, 2009) by POKE London for Orange UK, tied to Orange's Glastonbury Festival sponsorship. Each year, a GPS-tagged real bull was placed in a real field at a secret UK location (not at Glastonbury). Users guessed which grid square the bull would occupy at 3pm each day, with correct guessers entering a prize draw for Glastonbury tickets.

**Confirmed years:** 2007 (original launch), 2008 (relaunched with hoof-step tracking), 2009 (bull named Desmond, with Twitter account @spotthebull). Whether it also ran in 2004–2006 (as per Iain's three-year claim which may suggest 2007–2009 or could be earlier) is not yet confirmed from public sources.

**Awards confirmed:** Two LIA 2008 Gold wins (Online Games; Best Use of Interactivity). Named #1 in Campaign's Top 10 Digital Ads of 2007.

**Cultural significance:** Recognised in 2025 as one of the 10 best promotional campaigns ever run in the UK. Cited as direct inspiration for the Walkers "When it Rains" campaign. An early, clear proof-of-concept for physical-digital real-time integration in advertising — what would now be called an IoT-connected campaign.

**Key named collaborator:** Oliver Wright (Executive Producer). Agency team beyond Iain Tait and Nik Roope is unconfirmed.
