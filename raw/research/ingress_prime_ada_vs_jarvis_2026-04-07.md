## 1. Campaign Mechanics — Tool of North America Case Study

**URL:** https://toolofna.com/work/ai/ingress-prime-ada-vs-jarvis/
**Status:** LIVE. Full case study page, verified 2026-04-07.
**Credit line on Tool site:** "Wieden + Kennedy London" / "Director — Tool Experience"

### The Brief
To promote the release of Ingress Prime, Niantic Labs wanted to imagine the future of Artificial Intelligence in a campaign to recruit new players to the game. Tool of NA created ADA vs. JARVIS — "a multi-platform story that blurs the line between the exciting fiction and sobering reality of what is possible in AI."

### The Two AIs
- **ADA** — AI aligned with the Resistance faction (blue team)
- **JARVIS** — AI aligned with the Enlightened faction (green team)
- Both AIs "started life trained on only a small amount of data and would need to learn much more to be able to speak to the people and create propaganda to inspire them to join the cause."
- Both AIs "began conversations with the community to learn, and over time, they evolved their ability for language."

### Scale
- "The overall experience reached millions of people around the world"
- "Hundreds of thousands of them contributed to the training and participated in the experience"
- "2 AIs grew up before our eyes"

### Technical Ecosystem — Full Detail

Tool of NA "designed and built an entire ecosystem" consisting of:

**1. Natural Language Generation (NLG)**
- NLG is "a subfield in AI that focuses on imitating human writing using a neural network trained on a large corpus of curated text"
- Used to give each AI a voice — they created headlines for propaganda visuals and filled out chat interactions
- "The system was constructed to continually learn by harvesting text from chat interactions causing our AIs' voices to evolve and, over time, more and more closely resemble the speaking style of their faction"

**2. Chat Interactions**
- Users could directly interact with each AI
- "These training sessions gave users a way to directly interact with and influence the growth of the AI, and by translation, help the cause of their faction"

**3. Personality Graphs**
- Each AI had a "personality graph" — a simplified representation of AI personality as a graph of traits
- "This 'personality graph' was incrementally trained in real time by using a simple gradient descent type function"
- Training data came from "specific multiple choice image questions within the chat experience (i.e. which of these images best represent the faction?)"
- "These answers were delivered to the personality graph system in real-time, mid conversation, causing the personality to be a living breathing, always changing aspect to each AI"
- The personality graph influenced all image selection and content generation

**4. Parameterized Video / Content Generation**
- "The content generator was the engine that outputted all imagery in the campaign"
- Process: ingested current personality graph + fresh NLG headlines + content configuration
- "Assembled and delivered a range of generated images as options, which were then curated before being sent into the wild"
- "Happened multiple times per week throughout the campaign"
- "Thousands of images and background videos were curated and prepped to be in the resource pool"
- Each image was tagged with its own personality graph values so closest-match assets were selected
- The AIs "generated and posted hundreds of propaganda visuals on social media"

**5. Reinforcement System**
- After a propaganda image was "in the wild, it was looped back into the training chat experience in a multiple choice question"
- "As users voted for one piece of propaganda content over another, a simple neural network was trained on the 'successful' values of certain image properties"
- "Over time, this system helped optimize the effectiveness of the propaganda content"

**6. Campaign Controller**
- "A full-featured admin tool for the client to schedule, script, moderate, publish and test each piece of the campaign"
- "From here all significant dates in the timeline, content and copy for the website, content configuration for the content generator and chat scripts were stored and managed"
- "Testing tools for critical services are housed in the campaign controller. The moderation and publish systems are made available. Even the entire collection of accepted and rejected content are housed here"
- Designed so client could "potentially even add chapters for the story to continue into the future"

### Key Quote from Tool of NA
"With the combination of these approaches the generated propaganda content was often unsettling and compelling, drawing users in with unexpected and stirring combinations of words, imagery and effects."

## 6. Game Context and Status of Ingress Prime

### Ingress Prime Launch
- Launched: November 5, 2018
- Business Insider launch article: https://www.businessinsider.com/niantic-launches-ingress-prime-revamped-version-first-game-2018-11
- Variety relaunch article (Nov 5 2018): https://variety.com/2018/digital/news/niantic-ingress-prime-1203019560/
- Game Informer announcement (Dec 2017): https://gameinformer.com/b/news/archive/2017/12/04/pok-233-mon-go-developers-announce-ingress-prime-for-2018.aspx

### What Variety Said About "Ahead of Its Time"
Variety (Nov 2018) noted: "Ingress was also clearly ahead of its time. Combining mobile game-play with real-world locations and actions was a new idea, and the fact that 'Ingress' did so with a fairly complex science fiction story didn't exactly help to explain it to the masses."

This quote refers to the original Ingress game, not specifically to the W+K campaign, but is relevant context.

### Ingress Prime AI Characters in Game
From Variety: "'Ingress Prime' aims to do better by using two AI characters that help to make players familiar with the game's core mechanics and objectives." This confirms ADA and JARVIS were integrated into the game onboarding as well as the campaign.

### Ingress Redacted Shutdown
- The original Ingress client was renamed "Ingress [Redacted]" when Prime launched
- "Ingress [Redacted]": November 2012 - September 2019 (shut down September 30, 2019)
- Source: killedbyniantic.com — https://killedbyniantic.com/
- Reddit thread on shutdown: https://www.reddit.com/r/Ingress/comments/cx9hr3/redacted_leaving_september_30/
- The W+K campaign ran from November 2018 and was cut short when the broader Ingress community contracted sharply after the Redacted client shutdown and Niantic refocused resources

### Current Status of Ingress Prime (2026)
- Ingress Prime still technically live as of 2026 but described as "Limping Towards Death" on killedbyniantic.com
- "Not Dead Yet" — continues to operate though Niantic sold/wound down many other titles

### Reddit Community Reception (November 2018)
From Reddit r/Ingress Moronic Monday thread (November 19, 2018):
- https://www.reddit.com/r/Ingress/comments/9ydnti/moronic_monday_11192018_your_weekly_questions/
- One commenter said: "So I saw an ad for this on YouTube and I'm interested in AI. But what is this? Jarvis is an idiot. Why is there an advertisement for a brain dead chatbot..."
- This confirms: (a) the ADA/JARVIS chatbot ads were running on YouTube in November 2018; (b) some community members found the chatbots unsophisticated
