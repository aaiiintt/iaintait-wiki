## What the Campaign Was

### The Concept

Wieden+Kennedy London devised a campaign to prove that Pokémon GO was "still alive" after the massive launch hype had peaked. The strategy was to demonstrate the scale of the global community by challenging players worldwide to catch a collective target of **3 billion Pokémon in 7 days**.

To make this tangible and emotionally compelling, W+K worked with Niantic to send three social media influencers to Japan — the spiritual home of Pokémon — to document the journey and inspire the global player community.

The influencer trip and video series were called **"Pokémon GO Travel"**, and were positioned as a new ongoing video series: "a new video series to see the world through the eyes of a Pokémon Trainer." Japan was the first episode.

The in-game challenge was called the **"Global Catch Challenge"**.

### The Goal

- **3 billion Pokémon caught collectively by all players worldwide in 7 days**
- Three milestone reward tiers:
  - 500 million catches unlocked → first reward tier
  - 1.5 billion catches unlocked → second reward tier
  - 3 billion catches unlocked → full reward tier

### The Result

Players **succeeded**. The final count: **3.36 billion Pokémon caught in under 7 days**, by trainers in more than **140 countries**.

Progress tracking:
- Day 1 (Nov 20): 298 million caught
- Nov 21: 500 million milestone passed
- Nov 22: 1.5 billion passed
- Nov 23: 2 billion passed
- Nov 24: 2.5 billion passed
- Nov 25: 3 billion passed (target hit with one day to spare)

Final confirmed total: **3.36 billion Pokémon** (per Niantic's official recap post, November 30, 2017)

### Rewards Unlocked

1. **Farfetch'd** — appeared worldwide for 48 hours (normally a Japan-exclusive regional Pokémon)
2. **Kangaskhan** — appeared in East Asia for 48 hours
3. **Ho-Oh** — appeared in Legendary Raid Battles globally for the first time ever (available until December 12, 2017)

## W+K's Role

Wieden+Kennedy London created the overall campaign concept. This is confirmed by:

1. The Cannes Lions Work database (lovethework.com) listing "Pokémon GO Travel - Global Catch Challenge" as a W+K campaign (campaign ID 417572)
2. The 2018 Webby Award entry: entrant listed as **Wieden + Kennedy** — category: Best Community Engagement, PR Campaigns
3. The CD's firsthand account of W+K's involvement

**Note on W+K's exact scope vs. Niantic:** The YouTube video series (filmed content of the influencers in Japan) was produced with Niantic. W+K's role appears to have been conceiving the overall campaign strategy (the community challenge, the influencer trip, the narrative of "the game is still alive"), while Niantic's in-house team managed some of the day-to-day social content. This is typical of how influencer/brand-content hybrid campaigns work. The case study film (vimeo.com/289157518) uploaded by Peter Browse in September 2018 suggests the case study was W+K London's.

## The Scale and Impact

- **3.36 billion Pokémon caught** across 140+ countries in under 7 days (a record at the time)
- **Tottori Safari Zone:** 89,000 trainers attended the in-person closing event; 12 million Pokémon caught at the event
- **Tourism revenue for Tottori Prefecture:** US$16 million generated in 3 days (source: pocketgamer.biz)
- Game had **750+ million downloads** since July 2016 launch; **500+ million visits** driven to sponsored partner locations (e.g. Starbucks)
- Challenge reached 298 million catches on day one alone (November 20, 2017)
