The D&AD, LBB, Campaign, and other trade press do not appear to have covered this project — it was an internal/reception installation, not a client campaign.
- **derekandtomas.com/Headmaster** — Page is listed as a source URL in the project frontmatter but content was inaccessible in this research pass (WebFetch denied; no indexable content returned by WebSearch). Future retrieval attempt with Apify rag-web-browser or curl recommended.
- **Awards** — No award entries found. As an internal agency installation (not a client campaign), it is unlikely to have been entered at D&AD, Cannes or One Show. Could theoretically appear at Webby Awards (interactive), FWA, or similar digital/experiential shows — not confirmed.
- **"Full Force Wolf Horse" music** — Music credit in the existing project file. No further information found about this artist/band in research — may be an independent or emerging act, or an internal W+K music commission.
- **Jonathan Plackett's role** — The existing file lists him as a team member. His broader profile (confirmed in collaborators/) suggests a technologist/developer role at W+K London, which fits the project's technical nature.
- **Marc Winklhofer's spelling** — Project file notes "also spelled 'Mark' on derekmanlui.com." Research confirms both spellings appear. "Marc" is used in the project file; "Mark" appears in some source references. The derekmanlui.com source (the closest to primary) uses "Mark."
- **No trade press coverage found** — Searches for "Headmaster W+K London holographic arcade" returned only the derekmanlui.com page and unrelated results. This project appears to have generated no trade press coverage, consistent with it being an internal reception installation rather than a published campaign.
- **Iain Tait's role title** — The project file credits Iain Tait as "Creative Director" (not ECD, which is his formal title at W+K London). This is consistent with his more hands-on, personally-driven involvement in this project versus his oversight role on client campaigns.
