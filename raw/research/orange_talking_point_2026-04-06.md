### What the Site Did
Per Iain Tait's own account and the Auriol Clarke LinkedIn snippet: a debate/discussion platform for Orange UK users that used an innovative click-to-participate interface — an early implementation of what we would now recognise as a tag cloud or word cloud mechanic. Users could click on popular topics/words rather than having to write a full comment, solving the short-attention-span problem for online participation. This was a genuine interface innovation for the time (pre-tag-cloud terminology becoming mainstream).

## Dimension 6: Cultural Legacy (early tag cloud / debate interface)

### Context: The State of Online Discussion in 2005–2006
- **Amazon reviews** existed but were long-form
- **Forums/message boards** required registration and were text-heavy
- **Blogs** were growing but participation required writing
- **YouTube** launched in 2005 — comments culture was not yet established
- **Twitter** did not exist until 2006
- **Tag clouds** as a visual metaphor were just emerging — del.icio.us popularised them c.2003–2004, but the *interactive tag cloud as a participation mechanic* was novel
- **Word clouds** as a term were not yet common vocabulary

### What Talking Point Innovated
The innovation was using a popularity-weighted, clickable interface for public debate participation — essentially: "here are the things people are talking about, sized by how popular they are; click to add your voice." This solved two problems simultaneously:
1. **Participation friction:** No typing required — just click
2. **Legibility of public opinion:** The visual weight showed consensus at a glance

This predates what we'd now call "upvoting," "reaction buttons," or emoji responses by many years. It's a genuinely early example of the design pattern that became ubiquitous.

### In the Auriol Clarke LinkedIn framing:
*"Talking Point was an early dabble in social media"* — This is the client-side characterisation. The Webby citation's phrase "Pioneering social Media for Brand Engagement" further supports this framing.

## Summary

**Orange Talking Point** was a debate/discussion microsite created by POKE London for Orange UK, probably launched in late 2005 or early 2006. It won a **Webby Award in the Telecommunications category at the 10th Annual Webby Awards (May 2006)** — specifically cited for *"Pioneering social Media for Brand Engagement."*

The project's significance lies in its interface innovation: a click-to-participate mechanic using a popularity-weighted visual display of debate topics, which prefigured the tag cloud / word cloud as an interaction pattern and anticipated the participatory social web by several years.

The project was one of two Webbys POKE won in 2006 (alongside the Jamie Oliver Feed Me Better campaign), and was cited as the creative highlight of the POKE/Mother digital partnership in Campaign magazine's September 2006 feature on UK agency digital capabilities.

No video, screenshots, or archived versions of the live site have been located. The Webby Awards gallery entry exists but requires registration.
