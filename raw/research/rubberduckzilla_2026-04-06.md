## Confirmed Facts

### Campaign Overview
- **Client:** Oasis (UK soft drink, a Coca-Cola brand) — "for people who don't like water"
- **Campaign name:** Rubberduckzilla
- **Year:** 2009 (TV launched 18 May 2009, AR site launched ~June 2009)
- **Campaign concept:** A giant, water-hating rubber duck stomping through a Japanese city (Godzilla/King Kong pastiche)

### Agency Split — CRITICAL
- **TV ad, print, outdoor:** Mother London (NOT POKE)
  - TV Director: Joseph Kahn
  - Production Company: HSI
  - TV first aired during Hollyoaks on 18 May 2009
- **Digital/AR/interactive components:** POKE London
  - Iain Tait: ECD
  - Gavin Fox: Creative (confirmed — Digiday 2013 article calls him "creative director at Poke")
  - Jason [surname TBC]: Creative (credited alongside Katie in "ghetto demo" video)
  - Katie [surname TBC]: Also involved in demo video
  - Flo Heiss (from Dare, not POKE): Quoted by Iain in the crackunit blog post: "Augmented Reality is the Quicktime VR of 2009"

### POKE's Digital Work (Confirmed from Iain's crackunit post, 12 June 2009)
1. **AR Games website** at rubberduckzilla.com
   - Multiple AR games; at launch only two of four sections were active
   - Game mechanic: users become RDZ and fire lasers from their eyes
   - Also: putting the AR marker on your head so your head becomes the duck's head
   - 8-bit Japanese-style graphics and music
   - Required webcam + printable AR markers (using FLARToolKit — Flash-based open source AR)
2. **The Sun newspaper special episode**
   - "A special with The Sun" — front cover of the newspaper replaced with animated destruction content using AR
   - Physical copy of The Sun = the AR marker (no printer needed)
   - Iain described this as his favourite part: "it puts the 'marker' in peoples' hands and doesn't rely on printers and stuff"
   - This was a media takeover / AR partnership with News International / The Sun

### Campaign Context
- Followed controversial "Cactus Kid" ads (banned by ASA in Oct 2008 for condoning underage sex/teenage pregnancy)
- Rubberduckzilla was Oasis's return to screens after that ban
- Client: Cathryn Sleight (referenced in Campaign Live snippet)
- Target audience: 20-something consumers

## Technology
- Flash-based AR using FLARToolKit (open source, community-built)
- Required webcam + printed AR markers, OR a physical copy of The Sun newspaper
- The Sun partnership was notably innovative as it avoided the printer dependency barrier
