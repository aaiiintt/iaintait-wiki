## 3. Creative Concept

**The "Dickensian epic" origin story:**

Set in 1869 Dickensian London — the year John James and Mary Ann Sainsbury opened their first shop on Drury Lane, Holborn. The film tells a "true-ish" origin story of Father Christmas.

**Full narrative synopsis (from LBB, Guardian, Radio Times):**
- Nicholas (Nick), an orphaned chimney sweep, is wrongly accused of stealing a clementine from the Sainsbury's table of produce on the street
- He is banished by his evil boss and exiled into a blizzard
- He is rescued by a kindly Sainsbury's worker / Mrs. Sainsbury
- Nicholas and Mrs. Sainsbury secretly place clementines (oranges) into the stockings of all the chimney sweeps
- They deliver coal to the naughty boss
- Nicholas walks away in a very familiar red and white outfit — revealing him as the origin of Father Christmas
- The film is populated with Dickensian characters: Fagin, a Javert-like policeman, ghosts, a cackling witch, orphaned children ("a mob of orphaned tykes")
- Modern Easter egg: a horse-drawn carriage with a "Zero Emissions" sign

**Campaign tagline:** "Helping make Christmas, Christmas since 1869"

**Client quote (Laura Boothby, Head of Broadcast Marketing, Sainsbury's):**
> "How can we tell the origin story of Father Christmas — like Batman — and turn him into a superhero?"

**W+K description:**
> "A Dickensian epic in which Mr and Mrs Sainsbury cross paths with a young chimney sweep by the name of Nick, who is destined for greatness... filled with injustice, heartbreak, a sprinkle of magic and a dusting of nonsense. After all, little things like truth and facts should never get in the way of a good story."

**Director Ninian Doff (Instagram):**
> "Amazing cast, crew and agency. Had a lot of joyous madness on this and so great to be allowed to put so much of my weird brain into such a massive job."

**Production:** Shot in September 2019 on location in Romania, where there was a pre-existing Victorian London set. This saved on CGI costs and avoided UK weather issues. The production wanted it "bright and dry" and added snow in post.

**Lengths:** TV version 90 seconds (launched Tuesday 12 November during ITV's Emmerdale), cutdowns on broadcast TV, full 2 minute 30 second version online only.

**Contextual note:** Launched after Armistice Day (11 November) — Sainsbury's always waits out of respect. The ad aired during the Brexit election campaign period.

## 8. Cultural/Contextual Notes

- The campaign was Sainsbury's 150th anniversary ad — 1869 was the year their first store opened on Drury Lane, Holborn
- BBC produced a six-part docuseries about Sainsbury's 150th anniversary ("Inside the Supermarket" on BBC One) and attended the ad's preview screening — unusually high editorial interest
- Sainsbury's deliberately launches after Armistice Day (11 November) each year
- The 2019 campaign aired during the UK general election campaign, giving its nostalgic, escapist tone additional resonance
- The campaign context: Sainsbury's Christmas 2014 ("1914" — WWI truce) is considered one of the most acclaimed Christmas ads ever made; 2019 was explicitly positioned as a return to cinematic heritage storytelling
- Muse by Clios noted it as "gorgeously crafted" but "not as emotionally affecting as the '1914' work"
