## Overview

**Campaign:** Pokémon GO "City Celebrations"
**Agency:** Wieden+Kennedy London (creative); W+K Portland (global media planning and buying)
**Client:** Niantic Labs
**Brief:** Create the first ever above-the-line campaign for Pokémon GO to celebrate the game's one-year anniversary.
**Launch date:** June 8, 2017
**Campaign type:** Digital and static out-of-home (OOH) / outdoor advertising; some digital OOH
**Cities at launch:** London, New York, Tokyo (more cities pledged for days and weeks ahead)
**Historical significance:** This was the first-ever above-the-line campaign for Pokémon GO.

---

## Concept

The dynamic campaign paid tribute to a selection of Pokémon GO's top "Trainer Cities" by showing near real-time stats about local gameplay activity on digital and static out-of-home posters. Billboards displayed city-specific data: the number of Pokémon caught locally since the game launched.

Many of the billboards were simultaneously activated as **PokéStops** inside the game, meaning that players in those cities could physically visit the billboard locations and collect in-game items — blurring the line between advertisement and gameplay in a way that was entirely novel in outdoor advertising.

## Stats Cited at Launch (confirmed, June 8, 2017)

Source: Niantic official blog post (nianticlabs.com/news/wk), confirmed also in The Drum, Creativepool, W+K London blog

- **New York:** 16 million Pokémon caught (as of day of announcement)
- **London:** 17.5 million Pokémon caught
- **Tokyo:** 32.2 million Pokémon caught

These were described as stats "just yesterday" (i.e., that day's count), emphasising the near-real-time nature of the data-driven creative.

Additional context from Creativepool (June 9, 2017):
- Pokémon GO had been downloaded **750 million times** in its first year
- The game still had **65 million active players** at time of campaign
- Pokémon GO achieved **7.5 million downloads in the US** in just one week after launch (July 2016)
- Nintendo's share price spiked **65%** due to the game's viral popularity

## Context and Industry Commentary

### The Drum (June 8, 2017) — John McCarthy
The article confirmed that W+K "has embraced outdoor advertising to promote the game where it is played, in the great outdoors" and described it as an "international campaign touching down in 'Top Trainer Cities' to make use of local, real time data gathered via the app."

The article included a tweet from journalist John McCarthy (@JohnGeeMcCarthy) photographing the London billboards:
> "Ad campaign from @WiedenKennedy and @NianticLabs. It's digital, outdoor and uses real time data. Happy birthday @PokemonGoApp" — June 8, 2017

### Creativepool (June 9, 2017) — Additional Context
- Confirmed that W+K Portland handled global media planning and buying
- Noted Pokémon GO Fest would be hosted in Chicago's Grant Park on July 22
- Confirmed European events planned June–September in partnership with Unibail-Rodamco shopping centres
- Japan: "Pikachu Outbreak" event in Yokohama
- Confirmed 750 million total downloads in first year; 65 million active players at campaign time

### Niantic Official Blog — Key Language
Niantic described the campaign as being "conceptualized by our new creative agency, Wieden+Kennedy" — confirming that:
1. W+K had just been appointed as Niantic's creative agency (this was their first public campaign together)
2. The relationship was announced simultaneously with the campaign

This campaign was the **first public work** from the W+K London × Niantic relationship. The agency appointment was announced on the same day as the campaign launch (June 8, 2017). Niantic's blog post explicitly framed it as introducing their "new creative agency" — making this a dual announcement of both the agency appointment and the campaign.
