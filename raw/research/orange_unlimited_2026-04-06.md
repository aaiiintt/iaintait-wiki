## Dimension 5: Iain and Nik's Roles

### Attribution

- **Iain Tait's own account** (per CLAUDE.md context): Iain had the original idea but Nik Roope did the craft/execution. This is a shared origination.
- **Campaign Annual 2007** credits list: Nik Roope is listed first, then McKenna, Gibson, Barnes, Crowle, Watz, Ryan. Iain Tait does **not** appear in the Campaign credits for this project, though he co-ran Poke.
- **Dezeen (Nov 2007):** Roope is quoted directly: *"Orange asked us to talk about their unlimited offers so we made them a never ending web page."*
- **Designboom (2009):** Roope presented the project as his work at Belgrade Design Week.
- **Campaign India (2008):** Roope presented it again at Kyoorius Design Yatra.

### Conclusion

Orange Unlimited was a **POKE London agency project**. The credited creative lead is **Nik Roope** (co-founder, CEO, co-creative director). The Campaign Annual 2007 creatives list does not include Iain Tait, though he co-ran Poke. Iain's contribution was at the ideation/origination level as co-founder; the execution/craft/public-face was Roope. Both deserve mention; neither should be sole-credited.

## Dimension 6: Museum / Permanent Collection

### CRITICAL FINDINGS — UPDATED

The earlier research pass found evidence that museum-collection claims were linked to Nik Roope's Plumen lightbulb, not Orange Unlimited. This second pass **confirms and partially revises** that finding.

**New finding (confirmed this pass):** Team member **Nicky Gibson** (credited on Orange Unlimited) has a personal portfolio website at `https://www.nickygibson.com/about` and `https://www.nickygibson.com/exhibitions-awards` which states:

> *"Nicky Gibson is a multi-award winning — MoMA and Design Museum exhibited — digital design director..."*
>
> **Exhibitions:** MoMA, New York — Design Museum, London — ICA, The Institute of Contemporary Arts.

Gibson's client list on that page includes: **"Orange (Unlimited / Balloonacy)"** — explicitly naming Orange Unlimited as Poke work under her credits.

Gibson also links directly to her MoMA artist page at `https://www.moma.org/artists/39782`.

**Interpretation:** Nicky Gibson — a credited collaborator on Orange Unlimited — has work exhibited at MoMA and the Design Museum. This **does not** necessarily mean Orange Unlimited itself is in a permanent collection (MoMA artist pages can include exhibition history, not just permanent collection acquisitions). However, Gibson's dual exhibition at MoMA and the Design Museum, combined with Orange Unlimited being in her client list, provides a **plausible basis for Iain's memory** that the project "is in some permanent collection."

It is possible that:
1. A component of the work (possibly the Marius Watz generative art element, or the project itself as interactive/net art) entered MoMA's or the Design Museum's collection or archive, with Gibson as the designer of record.
2. Gibson's MoMA listing covers other digital work.
3. The Design Museum exhibited Orange Unlimited as part of a broader digital design show.

## Summary

**Orange Unlimited / The Never-Ending Webpage** (official title: *"Unlimited — Good Things Should Never End"*) was a Flash-based interactive website created by **POKE London** for **Orange UK**, launched in **November 2007** as part of a £6m campaign to promote Orange's unlimited mobile offering. The concept was an infinite-scrolling webpage — illustrating "unlimited" through the experience of a page that never ends, populated with animated characters, generative art, and reactive sound design.

**Creative team (Campaign Annual 2007 credits):** Nik Roope, Derek McKenna, Nicky Gibson, Julie Barnes, Rex Crowle, Marius Watz, Nick Ryan. Iain Tait was co-founder and co-creative director; his contribution was at the origination level.

**Awards:** Webby Award 2008 (Design Aesthetic winner); Cannes Cyberlions (Silver — Telecomms and Microsites); ADC New York Gold Cube (Innovation); BIMA Overall Winner; LIA 2008 finalist. Multiple additional awards via the team's collective Poke credits. Named by Advertising Age and Campaign among top digital work of 2007.

**Museum collection:** Possibly exhibited at MoMA, New York and/or Design Museum, London — confirmed for team member Nicky Gibson who lists "MoMA and Design Museum exhibited" and includes Orange Unlimited in her client list. Manual verification of moma.org/artists/39782 and the Design Museum collection required to confirm whether Orange Unlimited specifically is the indexed work.

**Archive:** Wayback Machine has a capture from April 10, 2008 at `https://web.archive.org/web/20080410173222/http://unlimited.orange.co.uk/flash/go` (Flash-based, likely renders blank but the capture exists). Original URL was `http://unlimited.orange.co.uk`, with the project also accessible at `http://www.goodthingsshouldneverend.co.uk`.
