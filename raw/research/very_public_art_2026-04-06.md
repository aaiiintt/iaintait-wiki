## Summary of Findings

The "Very Public Art Exhibition" was a POKE London project for Selfridges (400 Oxford Street, London W1) that allowed members of the public to bid for British art via SMS from Selfridges' shop window. It ran in approximately early 2003 (Design Week article dated 3 April 2003). The project won awards in both 2003 (BIMA) and 2004 (One Show New York Gold), reflecting a submission/win cycle across two award years.

## What the Project Was

Based on assembled evidence:

1. **Format:** A physical art exhibition installed in Selfridges' famous Oxford Street shop windows
2. **Mechanic:** Members of the public could bid on pieces of British art (Brit Art) using SMS/mobile phone — directly from the street looking at the window display
3. **Significance:** This was 2003 — well before mobile commerce and m-ticketing were mainstream. Using SMS as a bidding/transaction mechanism for art in a retail window was genuinely ahead of its time
4. **"Very Public":** The exhibition was literally public-facing — visible from the street, participatory via the handset in your pocket
5. **Technology stack:** SMS-based bidding system, likely requiring a shortcode number displayed alongside each artwork
