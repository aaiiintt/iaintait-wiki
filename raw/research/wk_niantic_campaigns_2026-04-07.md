## 1. Pokémon GO "City Celebrations" (2017)

### Overview
- **Campaign name:** Pokémon GO City Celebrations
- **Type:** First-ever above-the-line campaign for Pokémon GO; global outdoor (OOH) and digital
- **Occasion:** One-year anniversary of Pokémon GO
- **Launch date:** Announced publicly **June 8, 2017**
- **Cities:** London, New York, Tokyo (initial launch); more cities planned for weeks ahead
- **Client/brief:** "Create the first ever above the line campaign for Pokémon GO to celebrate the game's one year anniversary." (W+K London case study)

### Concept
Dynamic digital and static out-of-home posters featuring near real-time stats on local Pokémon GO gameplay. Billboards displayed Pokémon caught per city/neighbourhood at the time of the campaign. Many billboards were simultaneously activated as in-game **PokéStops**, so players could collect items from them in the real world.

**Stats cited at launch (Niantic blog, June 8, 2017):**
- New York: 16 million Pokémon caught
- London: 17.5 million Pokémon caught
- Tokyo: 32.2 million Pokémon caught

### Media / Format
- Digital and static OOH posters
- Specific London format confirmed: Trafalgar Square (crosstrack 48-sheet noted in asset filename), 6-sheet formats visible in press materials
- New York: Legendary catches billboard visible in press materials; Yankees-format billboard present
- Video asset confirmed: `https://wklondon.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/London-Pokemon-Go-digital-outdoor.mp4`

## 2. Niantic Ingress Prime — "ADA vs. JARVIS" (2018)

### Overview
- **Campaign name:** "ADA vs. JARVIS" (also referenced as "Ingress Prime — ADA vs. JARVIS")
- **Agency:** Wieden+Kennedy London (creative); Tool of North America (technology/experience build)
- **Client:** Niantic Labs
- **Product:** Ingress Prime (relaunch of the original Ingress AR game)
- **Year:** 2018

### Concept
A multi-platform narrative experience promoting the relaunch of Ingress Prime. The campaign was built around the game's two existing factions:
- **ADA** (Artificial Intelligence — aligned with the Enlightened faction)
- **JARVIS** (Artificial Intelligence — aligned with the Resistance faction)

The fictional AIs began with limited data and needed to "learn" from real players to evolve. They engaged the player community in ongoing conversations to train their language capabilities. The AIs then generated and posted **propaganda visuals** on social media channels — these visuals evolved based on community feedback, creating a living system.

Players could "train" their AI by:
- Chatting with it (to improve its language)
- Answering multiple-choice image questions (to shape its "personality graph")
- Voting on propaganda images (a reinforcement system that optimised future content)

### Technical Build (Tool of North America)
The full ecosystem included:
- **Natural Language Generation (NLG)** — gave each AI a distinct textual voice
- **Personality graphs** — traits updated in real-time from user interactions
- **Content generator** — assembled propaganda imagery dynamically from personality state + NLG headlines
- **Reinforcement system** — trained on community votes, optimised imagery over time
- **Campaign controller** — admin tool for Niantic to schedule, moderate, script, and publish the campaign

### Scale / Metrics
- "The overall experience reached millions of people around the world, hundreds of thousands of them contributed to the training and participated in the experience, and 2 AIs grew up before our eyes." (Tool of NA case study)

### Named Credits
- **Agency:** Wieden+Kennedy London (creative lead)

## 3. Harry Potter: Wizards Unite — Global Launch Campaign (2019)

### Overview
- **Campaign name:** Harry Potter: Wizards Unite global launch campaign
- **Agency:** Wieden+Kennedy London
- **Clients:** Niantic, Warner Bros. Games / Portkey Games
- **Product:** Harry Potter: Wizards Unite (AR mobile game)
- **Campaign premise:** Treated the game's narrative ("The Calamity" — magic uncontrollably erupting into the real world) as genuine, real-world news. Players were addressed as actual members of the Statute of Secrecy Task Force.
- **Biggest media investment Niantic had made to date** (per wklondon.com)

### Campaign Structure — Three Phases

#### Phase 1: Social/CCTV Teasers (from January 2019)
Short films appearing on social channels from January 2019, showing "traces of magic" caught on Muggle CCTV cameras and mobile phones.

#### Phase 2: "The Trace Report" / "Calling All Wizards" Teaser Film
A rallying cry from the Ministry of Magic, styled as an official government report. Directed by **William McGregor** and **Jorge Montiel** (co-directors).
