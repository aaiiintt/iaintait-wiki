## Campaign 1: Dodge "Tent Event" — Invisible Monkey (2010)

### Summary

W+K Portland created a TV campaign for Dodge's "Tent Event" — a 60-day free test-drive promotion. The original spot featured a chimpanzee dressed in an Evel Knievel-style stuntman jumpsuit who walks up to a confetti detonator and sets off a puff of confetti over a row of Dodge vehicles. The voiceover — delivered deadpan by Michael C. Hall (of *Dexter*) — declares: "This event could not be more amazing. Oh, wait. There's a monkey. I stand corrected."

### Timeline of the PETA Controversy

- **July 2010:** The original spot runs on TV and gains YouTube views. PETA's primatologist Julia Gallucci contacts Dodge, citing the well-documented abuse and traumatic conditions suffered by young chimpanzees used in entertainment. The chimp in the spot was named **Suzy** (also spelled "Suzie" in some sources). PETA notes Suzy also appeared in a *Drop Dead Diva* episode, where a whistleblower alleged on-set abuse by her trainer.
- **July 15, 2010:** PETA issues a public statement demanding removal of the ad: "Dodge isn't going to dodge a bullet on this one."
- **July 20, 2010:** Dodge CEO informs PETA the chimp will be removed. Dodge head of communications Kristin Starnes confirms: "We are planning to modify the ads to eliminate the chimpanzee part." Plans for a companion print ad campaign featuring the chimp are also cancelled.
- **August 13, 2010:** The revised "Invisible Monkey" spot airs. The chimp is digitally removed and replaced with an obviously empty space. The voiceover is rerecorded with Hall saying: "This event could not be more amazing. Oh, wait. There's an invisible monkey. Unbelievable." The CGI blank was intentionally conspicuous — the joke being the absurdity of an invisible monkey.

### The NMA Angle

To steer media conversation away from a "middle finger to PETA" narrative, W+K secretly commissioned Next Media Animation World News (NMA) — the Taiwanese company famous for animated internet news — to tell the story of the invisible monkey "in an organic way." NMA produced the piece in approximately five hours. The collaboration between W+K and NMA was kept confidential at the time. The strategy worked: press framing shifted from outrage to amusement, and PETA expressed satisfaction.

### Creative Details

- **Campaign name:** Dodge Tent Event / Invisible Monkey Campaign
- **Client:** Dodge (Chrysler Group)
- **Agency:** Wieden+Kennedy Portland
- **Voiceover artist:** Michael C. Hall (*Dexter*)
- **Chimp:** Suzy (chimpanzee)
- **Concept:** 60-day free test-drive promotional campaign with a "tent sale" visual identity — balloons, confetti, giant red-and-white tent — plus a chimpanzee as the kicker
- **Named credits (individual writer/AD/director):** Not identified in available sources. The Adweek piece (Tim Nudd, "Freak Week: Monkey Business") and the thisisnotadvertising.wordpress.com case study both reference W+K Portland as a whole without naming individual creatives.

## Campaign 2: Coca-Cola / Maroon 5 — "Coca-Cola Music 24hr Session" (2011)

### Summary

Under the "Coca-Cola Music" brand umbrella, Coca-Cola partnered with US rock band Maroon 5 to write and record a completely original track in 24 hours, with the session streamed live to a global internet audience. Fans worldwide could contribute lyrics, beats, and comments in real time via social media; their submissions were projected onto the studio walls via a large-scale projection-mapped interactive sculpture. The event took place in a London studio on **22 March 2011**, starting at 17:00 GMT. W+K managed the campaign across W+K Portland and W+K London offices.

### Campaign Name & Song

- **Campaign name:** "Coca-Cola Music 24hr Session" (also referred to simply as "#WithMaroon5" on social media)
- **Song title:** "Is Anybody Out There" (also written as "Anybody Out There"), featuring **PJ Morton**
- **Available on YouTube:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IJdWn96ldOE (fan upload, 95K+ views, tagged "Maroon 5 ft PJ Morton - Is Anybody Out There? (Coca Cola 24hr Session)")
- For first 100,000 downloads, Coca-Cola donated to The Coca-Cola Africa Foundation's Replenish Africa Initiative (RAIN) to fund clean water access across Africa.

### Event Details

- **Date:** 22 March 2011, 17:00 GMT — running 24 hours
- **Location:** London studio (specific studio name not confirmed in sources)
- **Live-stream platform:** coca-cola.com/music microsite
- **Reach:** Live-stream reached **139 countries** in 24 hours
- **Social promotion:** Promoted to 20+ million consumers via Coca-Cola Facebook page (26 million "likes" at time of event); Coca-Cola Twitter account (@CocaCola, 277,000 followers) used for real-time updates; bloggers from nearly 20 countries attended in-studio
- **Fan interaction mechanisms:** Twitter submissions curated and projected onto studio walls; gesture-based interaction allowing band members and event MC to interact with incoming content in real time; hashtag votes (e.g., #james, #jessie, #adam to vote on guitarist performance)

### Technology & Interactive Installation

- **Commissioned from:** Nexus Interactive Arts / directing duo **Hellicar & Lewis** (Pete Hellicar and Joel Lewis)
- **System:** A large projection-mapped 3D sculpture placed in the centre of the studio, built using openFrameworks; harvested content from social media and projected it in a way that could be interacted with via gestures by band members and the event MC (named Kim)
- **Live broadcast:** Custom broadcasting system also designed in-house at Hellicar & Lewis
- **Collaborators on the installation (from Hellicar & Lewis portfolio):**
  - Chris Sugrue
  - James George
  - Josh Nimoy
  - Joshua Noble
  - Marek Bereza
  - Todd Vanderlin
- Maroon 5's reaction on being introduced to the sculpture: "I feel like Tom Cruise in Minority Report" and "This is cool, we should have one on tour."

- Band wrote and recorded a full track from scratch within 24 hours
- Song downloaded by fans globally; charitable component (RAIN clean water initiative)
- "Tens of thousands of people around the globe" watching late-night stream
