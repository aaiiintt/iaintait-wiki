## 2. Launch Date

### WIRED Article — Primary Source
- **URL:** https://www.wired.com/2003/09/website-westerners-pretty-rich/
- **Author:** Leander Kahney
- **Published:** 11 September 2003
- **Key excerpts:**
  - "Since going live last Monday [i.e., ~Monday 1 September 2003], the site has attracted 120,000 unique visitors."
  - "Roope said the site was launched to coincide with this week's World Trade Organization summit in Cancun, Mexico" — the WTO Cancun summit ran 10–14 September 2003, meaning the site launched in the week of 1–8 September 2003.
  - "Nicolas Roope, one of the site's creators" — "The site was created by Roope and several others from Poke, a London interactive media company."
  - "Roope, 32, Poke's creative director..."
  - "Roope said he and his colleagues dreamed up the idea for the site last year [i.e., 2002]. They shopped it around to several charities but none was interested without changing it significantly to fit their campaigns. Reluctant to adapt the idea, Poke launched the site on its own. It soon caught the eye of Care International, which asked to be associated with it."
  - Initial donations: "donations total about $1,700" in the first week.
  - "The idea is to really make people think about how rich they are compared to the rest of the world. In the West, we tend to obsess about celebrities and the super-rich. This is a really simple way to turn that on its head."
  - Initial viral spread: "the site topped the weblog popularity indices — Daypop, Blogdex, Popdex — late last week."
  - Press coverage at launch: London Guardian, USA Today.

### Cool Hunting 2013 Article
- Describes it as "Ten years after their launch during the 2003 G8 Summit" — note: the G8 Summit in 2003 was held in Evian, France, 1–3 June 2003. This may be a slight inaccuracy in Cool Hunting's write-up; the WIRED article is a more contemporaneous primary source linking launch to the WTO Cancun summit (September 2003).

**Conclusion: Launch date is September 2003 (approx. week of 1–8 September 2003).**

## 9. What the Site Does (for wiki description)

From WIRED (2003):
> "You simply plug in your annual income, and the site tells you where you rank among the world's richest people. For example, individuals in the United States who make less than $9,300 are officially poor... but compared with the rest of the world, their income is in the top 12 percent. An annual household income of $42,200 — the U.S. median in 2001 — is enough to land someone in the world's richest 1 percent."

From Third Sector (2005):
> "The viral, inspired by The Sunday Times' annual rich list, asks users to enter their income and reveals their position on a slide indicator of the personal wealth of the world's population. There is an option to donate."
