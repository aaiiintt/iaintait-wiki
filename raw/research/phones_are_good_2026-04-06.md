## Dimension 4: Metrics

- **Three customer data usage at launch:** 7.6GB/month average — 3.5x more than the UK industry average of 1.9GB/month (Ofcom 2017 data)
- **Business result:** Approximately £189 million incremental revenue uplift (source: Selfstorming analysis, citing IPA effectiveness data)
- **Brand problem it solved:** At launch, 40% of Millennials considered Three a "bad network" — campaign drove record-high consideration levels
- **Jennifer Aniston Instagram record (related activation, Oct 2019):** As a continuation of the "Phones Are Good" platform/brand positioning, Three collaborated with Jennifer Aniston for her 2019 Instagram debut. She reached 1 million followers in 5 hours 16 minutes — a Guinness World Record (Guinness World Records confirmed: https://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/news/2019/10/friends-star-jennifer-aniston-claims-record-for-fastest-to-reach-one-million-inst-595437)

## Dimension 6: Cultural Legacy

1. **Advertising industry recognition:** Cited by multiple industry creatives as one of the best mobile/telco ads of the era. Named by Kenneth Moore (We Are Social) in a June 2021 The Drum roundup as "easily the most unforgettable phone ad for me" from the decade.

2. **Contextual boldness:** Campaign launched at a cultural moment of intense anti-smartphone sentiment (digital detox movement, phone bans in schools, #screentime anxiety). Three and W+K deliberately positioned against this as a "Backlash Brand."

3. **Sequel/legacy:** The 2020 campaign "Real 5G" (also directed by Ian Pons Jewell for W+K London) is explicitly framed in press as a continuation of the Phones Are Good platform. Campaign Live called it "a joyous 'Phones Good' sequel."

4. **Valentine's Day extension (Feb 2019):** The hero film was recut with a Tinder/romance focus and ran across OOH, digital and social. Three's Oxford Street flagship was filled with thousands of roses, and 45 stores had themed window features.

5. **Artistic activation:** London painter Reuben Dangoor — known for painting grime artists in 17th-century grandeur style — was commissioned to reimagine historical figures through a modern social-media-profile lens. These were exhibited at 13 Soho Square, London, 26–27 October 2018. Retail stores across Glasgow, Belfast, Manchester, Liverpool, Cardiff and London also hosted gallery-style exhibitions from 17 October.

6. **Phone Spa activation:** In-store "Phone Spa" experiences (screen cleaning, protectors) rolled out in Three stores from end of October 2018.

7. **Still cited in 2025:** A Campaign Live "3 great ads I had nothing to do with" column (July 2025) features Phones Are Good as one of the three picks.

## Summary

**"Phones Are Good"** was a full-scale brand campaign for Three UK, launched 17 October 2018 by Wieden+Kennedy London. The hero asset is a 2:33 time-travel comedy film directed by **Ian Pons Jewell** through production company **Friend London**, with VFX by Time Based Arts, edit by Cut & Run, and sound by 750mph.

The creative team was **Tom Bender** (Art Director) and **Tom Corcoran** (Copywriter), working under Creative Director **Hollie Walker** and Executive Creative Directors **Iain Tait** and **Tony Davidson**.

The campaign was far broader than just the film: OOH and press nationwide, Valentine's Day extension (Feb 2019), a commissioned art gallery activation by painter Reuben Dangoor at 13 Soho Square, retail store takeovers, and in-store Phone Spa experiences.

It won a **One Show 2019** award and **Creative Circle** awards for Best VFX/CGI and Best Grade. It delivered approximately **£189 million in incremental revenue** and drove record-high brand consideration. It spawned a direct sequel campaign — "Real 5G" (2020) — also directed by Ian Pons Jewell.

The campaign is consistently cited by industry creatives as one of the best telco/mobile ads of its era and continues to appear in "greatest ads" roundups as recently as 2025.
