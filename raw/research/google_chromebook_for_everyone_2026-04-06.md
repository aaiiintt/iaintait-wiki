# Google Chromebook "For Everyone" Campaign — Research Notes
**Project:** Google Chromebook "For Everyone"
**Client:** Google
**Agencies:** Google Creative Lab (in-house) + Gretel (design/OOH) + 72andSunny (later campaign work)
**Year:** 2012–2013 (launch film October 2012; Times Square activation Thanksgiving November 2012; 72andSunny engagement from February 2013)
**Research Date:** 2026-04-06

---

## 1. Campaign Name & Tagline — CONFIRMED

**Campaign name: "For Everyone"** — confirmed by multiple primary sources.

The tagline is **"For Everyone"** — this appears directly in the TV spot as a title card, and was explicitly named as the campaign by Sundar Pichai at launch.

- TechCrunch (October 18, 2012): *"Google To Launch 'For Everyone' Ad Campaign Around New Chromebooks, Expands Best Buy Partnership To 500 Stores"*
  - URL: https://techcrunch.com/2012/10/18/chromebooks-for-everyone/
  - Pichai quote: "Google is launching an ad campaign around that theme, starting with TV spots that should air tonight."

- NYT Bits Blog (October 24, 2012): *"Google Shifts Pitch for Its New Chromebooks"*
  - URL: https://archive.nytimes.com/bits.blogs.nytimes.com/2012/10/24/google-shifts-its-pitch-for-chromebooks/
  - "In the new ads for the latest Chromebooks... the tagline is, 'For everyone.'"
  - "Two years ago... the tagline at the end of the ad was, 'Ready when you are.'"

- Official Google Blog post on the launch was titled: "The new Chromebook, for everyone"
  - URL (referenced in LA Times): https://googleblog.blogspot.com/2012/10/the-new-chromebook-for-everyone.html

The on-screen superimposed captions in the TV spot read: "FOR LAZY SUNDAYS", "FOR HOMEWORK", "FOR GOO", "FOR WORKING AT HOME", "FOR MOVIE FRIDAYS" — all using the "FOR..." construct.

## 2. Specific Executions

### TV Spot — "For Everyone" (launch film)

- **YouTube URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S95J5BowMmk (referenced in TechCrunch and NYT articles)
- Also on YouTube as: "Google - Chromebook - For Everyone (Advert Jury)" — https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ba2Tfm0dyNY
- **Description:** Home video style footage of family life. No specs or tech features mentioned. Shows Chromebook at kitchen table ("for homework"), on the deck ("for working at home"), in the kitchen ("for goo"), in bed ("for lazy Sundays"), on the couch ("for movie Fridays"). Brief glimpse of a toddler stepping on one.
- **Music:** The Death Set (band). Per jakedubs.com: *"And at long last, The Death Set, the band behind the backing track, finally got to sell out."*
- **Aired:** October 18, 2012 evening (during baseball playoffs per NYT)

### TV Spot — "Goodbye to All That"

- **YouTube URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PRTI_Z9POyo
- Title confirmed from YouTube search results: "Chromebook: For Goodbye To All That"
- This appears to be a second spot in the campaign

### OOH / Times Square Takeover (Thanksgiving 2012)

- **Source:** Gretel NY case study — https://gretelny.com/google-times-square
- **Timing:** Thanksgiving holiday weekend, November 2012
- **Format:** Takeover of **9 of Times Square's biggest screens** for 3 days
- **Mechanic:** Users could upload their own images with a "For…" caption and see themselves on screen — "gallery for everyone" interactive element
- **Challenge:** Each of the 9 locations was a unique format, ratio and size. Gretel created "a consistent language of animation that could then be customised to take advantage of the different screens." The design accommodated both Chromebook messaging and user-submitted content.
- **Coverage:** Engadget, November 25, 2012 — *"Google's 'gallery for everyone' lets you take over Times Square"* — https://www.engadget.com/2012/11/25/googles-gallery-for-everyone-lets-you-take-over-times-square/
  - "Submit a picture at the source link, along with a snappy 'For...' caption, and you could find yourself, your mom, your cat, or football team up there for all to see."

### Digital / Facebook GIFs (later execution — ASUS Chromebook Flip)

- **Source:** Alex Spott portfolio — http://www.hialexspott.com/
- Two Facebook GIF executions for the ASUS Chromebook Flip (a later "For Everyone" campaign iteration):
  - "Treat yo' self to an ASUS Chromebook Flip. A laptop and tablet in one. From $249."
  - "The ASUS Chromebook Flip for work and play. A laptop and tablet in one. From $249."
- **Note:** This appears to be a continuation or refresh of the "For Everyone" platform for a later model — the ASUS Chromebook Flip is a different device from the 2012 Samsung Chromebook launch.

## 3. Gretel's Role

**Source:** https://gretelny.com/google-times-square

Gretel (NYC-based design and branding studio, founded 2005) was responsible specifically for:

- **The Times Square visual system:** designing the animation language for 9 different screens across Times Square for the Thanksgiving 2012 activation.
- Creating a "consistent language of animation" that could be customised for each unique screen format, ratio and size.
- Design that accommodated both static Chromebook brand messaging AND dynamic user-submitted photo content.
- They did NOT create the TV spot — that was Google Creative Lab directly.

**Gretel's Principal/ECD at the time:** Greg Hahn (per imageofthestudio.com profile)

**Gretel summary quote from their case study:** "The Google Chromebook is a revolutionary laptop priced to be accessible to anyone. To celebrate the launch, Google staged a takeover of 9 of Times Square's biggest screens for the Thanksgiving holiday weekend. A key platform of the 'For Everyone' campaign, the event encouraged users to upload their own images along with a 'For…' caption and find themselves, their friends, their family or their cat up onscreen."

## 4. 72andSunny's Role

**Key finding:** 72andSunny was NOT involved in the October 2012 launch of the campaign. They were brought on in **February/March 2013** to work on **Google Chrome** (browser) — a related but distinct brief.

**Source:** Ad Age, February 22, 2013 — "72andSunny Picks Up Google Chrome Assignment"
- URL: https://adage.com/article/agency-news/72andsunny-picks-google-chrome-assignment/239983/
- Author: Shareen Pathak
- Published: February 22, 2013
- Context: Until then, much of Google Chrome brand messaging came from BBH (Publicis Groupe). Google typically uses a roster of shops on a project basis.
- The article notes Google spent $72.2 million on measured media for Chrome between January and November 2012.

**Mediapost confirmation:** https://www.mediapost.com/publications/article/194340/google-chrome-taps-72andsunny.html
- Published: February 26, 2013
- "72andSunny is working on a new campaign for Google Chrome."

**Alex Spott's portfolio** (hialexspott.com) shows her working on the ASUS Chromebook Flip "For Everyone" Facebook GIFs — she was at 72andSunny at the time. This suggests 72andSunny continued to work on Chromebook iterations after the Chrome browser brief.

**Important clarification on roles:**
- The **original October 2012 campaign** ("For Everyone" TV spot + Gretel Times Square) = Google Creative Lab in-house production
- **72andSunny** = came in February/March 2013 for Google Chrome browser work; also created later Chromebook iterations

**Named person at 72andSunny on Chromebook/Chrome work:**
- Alex Spott (writer/creative) — worked at 72andSunny from 2009–2011 per her bio, then freelanced at Google Creative Lab 2012 — this needs clarification. The ASUS Chromebook Flip work was 72andSunny work.
- NOTE: The original research brief may have conflated two phases: (a) the 2012 launch = Google Creative Lab, and (b) the 2013+ ongoing work = 72andSunny.

## 7. Which Chromebook Model

**The primary launch device:** Samsung Chromebook (Wi-Fi, 11.6") — $249
- Manufacturer: Samsung (running Google Chrome OS)
- Specs: Samsung Exynos 5250 processor (ARM-based, smartphone chip), 11.6" display (1366x768), 2GB RAM, 16GB SSD storage, 2.43 lbs, 0.8 inches thin
- USB 3.0 + USB 2.0, HDMI out, Bluetooth
- Battery: 6.5+ hours
- Includes: 100GB Google Drive storage free for 2 years
- Source: USA Today, October 18, 2012

**Also available at same time:** LG Chromebook at $349 (Wi-Fi)
- The "$249/$349" pricing mentioned in the brief confirms this dual-model launch

**Later models covered by "For Everyone" platform:**
- ASUS Chromebook Flip (laptop/tablet 2-in-1) — referenced in Alex Spott's 72andSunny work
- HP Chromebook 11 ($279) — launched October 2013

The original campaign film featured the Samsung Chromebook almost invisibly (per NYT: "relatively few shots of the Chromebook itself (there was a fleeting glimpse of a toddler stepping on one)").

## 9. Metrics & Impact

### Confirmed metrics

**From Gretel case study (https://gretelny.com/google-times-square):**
- "Millions of visitors passed through Times Square during those 3 days"
- "Website traffic spiked by over 50%"
- **"The Chromebook sold out in less than 24 hours"** (this refers to the Times Square activation's impact, likely Thanksgiving weekend)

**From jakedubs.com (primary source):**
- **"Chromebook sales hit more than 5 million in the first year alone"**
- "The spot appeared on the Official Google Blog the day of the launch"

**From NYT (strategy context):**
- Previous Chromebook pricing: $449 (Wi-Fi only) — new pricing $249 represented massive accessibility improvement
- Google described Chromebooks as a "family's second, at-home computer"
- Sundar Pichai: "Of the people who bought it, the most common use case is people just use this as an additional computer at home."

**Retail expansion:**
- TechCrunch noted Best Buy expanded from 100 to 500 stores at launch, with special Chromebook kiosks staffed by Google-trained specialists.
