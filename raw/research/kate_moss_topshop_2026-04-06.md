**Date:** 2026-04-06

## Summary of Findings

### 1. The Campaign

- **Client:** Topshop (Arcadia Group, owner Sir Philip Green)
- **Agency:** POKE London
- **Year:** 2007 — first collection launch previewed 30 April 2007 (Oxford Street overnight opening), officially on sale 1 May 2007
- **Scope:** Digital brand identity, microsite / interactive lookbook for the Kate Moss x Topshop collection launch
- POKE also built the **Topshop Fashion Fix Facebook application** (launched December 2007), per Campaign Live (3 Dec 2007)
- The microsite featured video podcasts showing Moss designing the range, blogs, and stills of the collection
- The collection ran continuously 2007–2010, with a final drop in 2014

### 2. Cultural Moment Confirmed

Multiple major press outlets confirm the Oxford Street phenomenon:

- **The Guardian** (1 May 2007): "Queue for preview sale of Moss Topshop collection — Store's guidelines avoid Primark-style scrum." Moss hosted a private champagne reception, then 150 shoppers at a time were admitted. The collection included a £12 vest top to a £150 leather jacket. Source: https://www.theguardian.com/uk/2007/may/01/fashion.retail
- **CNN** (1 May 2007): "Kate Moss wannabes mob London store" — "little could have prepared me for the madness Monday night." Five-item purchase limit imposed. Topshop reportedly paid Moss ~$6 million. Source: https://www.cnn.com/2007/SHOWBIZ/05/01/moss.clothing/index.html
- **TIME Magazine** (24 May 2007): "How Topshop Changed Fashion" — the Barneys NYC launch sold out in four hours. Green announced $100m investment in US expansion off the back of Moss launch momentum. Source: https://time.com/archive/6681138/how-topshop-changed-fashion/
- **CBS News** (1 May 2007): "Kate Moss' Designs Unveiled In London" — crowds queued overnight, colour-coded wristbands, 20-minute time slots, 50-piece collection. Source: https://www.cbsnews.com/news/kate-moss-designs-unveiled-in-london/
- **Getty Images**: Multiple photos archived from the Oxford Street launch (April/May 2007), including Kate Moss posing in the Topshop window.

### 3. POKE / Simon Waterfall Connection

**Confirmed POKE built the Topshop digital experience:**

- **Campaign Live** (3 Dec 2007) — "Topshop launches Facebook application" — explicitly names POKE: *"The branded application, developed by Poke, will sit alongside Topshop's existing ecommerce portal, which allows online shoppers to choose from over 3000 different fashion items. Topshop recently unveiled a branded microsite supporting the launch of its exclusive clothing range designed by supermodel Kate Moss. The retailer is used its digital channels to provide consumers with content including video podcasts showing Moss designing the range, blogs and stills of the collection."*
  Source: https://www.campaignlive.co.uk/article/topshop-launches-facebook-application/770872

- **Campaign Live** (article 773098 — "Dare scoops Digital Agency of the Year"): The search result snippet (article blocked at 403) confirms: *"topshop.com site that played a part in the successful launch of the Kate Moss line for the retailer. The creative director Simon Waterfall's appointment as..."*
  Source: https://www.campaignlive.co.uk/article/dare-scoops-digital-agency-year/773098 (paywalled/blocked)
  NOTE: This article concerns Dare winning Digital Agency of the Year — Simon Waterfall appears to have moved to/been mentioned in context of Dare, not POKE, for this particular award context. Requires further clarification. Simon Waterfall was Creative Director and Co-Founder of POKE. His CV at waterfall.co.uk confirms co-founder/CD of POKE.

**Simon Waterfall's POKE project page** (https://waterfall.co.uk/projects/poke):
- Lists Co-Founder & Creative Director of POKE
- Clients: "Oxfam to Orange, Nike to Nokia"
- Notes Alexander McQueen work (BAFTA-winning website) as headline project
- **Does NOT explicitly mention the Kate Moss / Topshop project** — the page focuses heavily on Alexander McQueen
- His awards page (https://waterfall.co.uk/cv/talks-awards) lists POKE awards 2009, 2008, 2006, 2005, 2004 — **conspicuously skips 2007**. The Webby Award for Topshop does NOT appear in his CV awards list.

### 4. Webby Award — Status: UNCONFIRMED

**Critical finding:** The claimed 2007 Webby Award for Kate Moss / Topshop **cannot be verified from available sources.**

- Simon Waterfall's Talks & Awards page (https://waterfall.co.uk/cv/talks-awards) lists POKE Webby wins for:
  - 2006: Orange Talking Point; Jamie Oliver "Feed me better"
  - 2005: Jamie Oliver (Best Celebrity site); Alexander McQueen (Best Fashion site)
  - **2007: No entry at all** — the list jumps from 2006 to the 2008/2009 section
- The Webby Awards website (webbyawards.com) returned a 404 for the 2007 fashion category URL. The winners gallery requires registration to search.
- No independent press source found confirming a Webby Award for Topshop/Kate Moss 2007.

**Assessment:** The Webby Award claim in the existing project file appears to be **unverified / possibly incorrect**. Simon Waterfall's own CV — the most authoritative source — does not list it. The award may be confused with the 2005 Webby for Alexander McQueen (Best Fashion site). Recommend flagging this as unconfirmed in the wiki and removing the claim or marking it with a caveat until primary evidence is found.

### 7. Collection Context

- First collection: April/May 2007 — ~50 pieces, £12 vest to £150 leather jacket. Inspired by Moss's own wardrobe.
- Sold in Topshop flagship Oxford Street and 20 countries; Barneys NYC sold out in 4 hours.
- Collection ran annually until 2010; final collection 2014.
- The iconic floral tea dress from the original 2007 collection remains highly collectible.
