## Additional research findings

### Campaign context: The Last Dance ESPN broadcast
The ad debuted during *The Last Dance*, the ESPN/Netflix docuseries about Michael Jordan and the 1997–98 Chicago Bulls, which aired in the US from April–May 2020 during the COVID lockdown. The docuseries was a major cultural moment — Facebook placing its "Still Going Strong" ad here was a high-profile and deliberate contextual choice. *The Last Dance* attracted some of the highest viewership numbers for ESPN since the 2012 NBA Finals.

### Production methodology: lockdown-made
The film is notable for being produced entirely during COVID-19 lockdown. It did not involve any traditional on-location production; all footage was sourced from real members of Facebook Groups (camping groups, cooking groups, yoga groups, gardening groups, technology communities). Director Maceo Frost (Knucklehead) assembled the campaign from submitted/sourced footage. This production methodology — user-generated/documentary footage assembled by a director — is an interesting creative/practical constraint worth noting for the wiki.

### "More Together" platform context
The ad is described as part of Facebook's "More Together" global brand platform. W+K Portland held the global Facebook account; W+K London's involvement (via David Dao as CD and Iain Tait/Tony Davidson as ECDs) reflects the agency's cross-office collaboration structure. This was W+K London's first TV commercial for Facebook despite the agency holding the account relationship for several years.
