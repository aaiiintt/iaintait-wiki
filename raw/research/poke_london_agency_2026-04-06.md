## 1. FOUNDING & HISTORY

### Founding Year: 2001 (confirmed)

Multiple independent sources confirm POKE was founded in **2001**, not 2000.

- The Guardian (Sep 2013 acquisition article): "Poke has garnered praise for its groundbreaking digital work and creative output since being founded in **2001** by Nicolas Roope, Nick Farnhill, Tom Hostler and Peter Beech."
- Marketing Week (Feb 2010 Iain Tait departure article): "Tait is joining Portland-based creative agency Wieden+Kennedy (W+K) after **eight years** at Poke, which he co-founded in **2001**."
- LBBOnline (Sep 2013 Publicis acquisition): "The agency, which was **founded in 2001**, is highly regarded for its award-winning work…"
- Simon Waterfall's CV at waterfall.co.uk: "**2001 - 2009 Poke / Founder & Creative Director**"
- Tom Hostler (Creative Review, Jan 2025): "co-founding Poke in **2001** alongside Nicolas Roope, Nick Farnhill, Peter Beech, Iain Tait and Simon Waterfall"

The earliest Wayback Machine capture of pokelondon.com is dated **18 Jan 2002** (the site was live by that date). The domain was registered and operational in early 2002 at latest, consistent with a late 2001 founding.

### The Oven Digital Connection

Nik Roope (LBBOnline, July 2012 interview) described the Oven Digital period directly:

> "I did a brief stint at a company before that called CHBi which turned into European Razorfish. Then I went onto a place called Oven Digital, which was an 18 month, crazy time… It grew up to 75 people in London; so I was running the office and creative director at the international group… and then I had to fire everybody, including myself all within 18 months."

And on founding POKE from Oven's ashes:

> "…out of the ashes of that, we formed Poke."

Simon Waterfall's CV lists:
- **1995–2001 Deepend & DeepGroup / Founder & Creative Director**
- **2001–2009 Poke / Founder & Creative Director**

Waterfall came from Deepend (a different dotcom agency), not Oven Digital — though the founding team included people from multiple crashed dotcom-era digital agencies.

The POKE pitch to themselves after the crashes was clearly articulated by Roope:

> "One thing that we were disappointed by was just how we ended up doing lots of work that we didn't believe in, and in a manner that we didn't think was very productive… So, when Iain Tait, Peter Beech, Nick Farnhill, Simon Waterfall and myself - and Tom Hostler started off, we just wanted to get back to basics and make the work that we thought was creatively interesting."

### The Mother London Connection

Simon Waterfall's website describes POKE as: "A digital company **grown within the advertising agency Mother** to learn, stretch and 'Poke' the finger at digital boundaries."

The Grokipedia article on Mother (advertising agency) confirms: Mother made "investments in complementary ventures like Naked and **Poke**."

The Guardian's 2013 acquisition article notes: Mother London "owns a **50.01% stake**" in POKE at time of acquisition. POKE was acquired by Publicis from "its four founding shareholders and Mother Holdings Ltd."

This establishes the structure: POKE was incubated/funded by Mother London, with the four named founding shareholders (Roope, Farnhill, Beech, Hostler) plus Mother as majority investor. Iain Tait and Simon Waterfall are also consistently named as co-founders in primary sources, suggesting the "four founding shareholders" referenced at acquisition were those remaining after Waterfall left in September 2009 and Tait announced departure in February 2010 (left March 2010).

### Office Location

The Guardian article on BakerTweet (April 2009) confirms POKE's **Shoreditch** location: "Implemented at the **Albion** cafe **across the road from Poke's Shoreditch headquarters**." The Albion café is on Boundary Street / Redchurch Street area of Shoreditch. Tom Hostler (Evening Standard, Nov 2013) wrote: "As an employer in the **creative and digital sector in Shoreditch**…"

Tom Hostler's 2025 Creative Review interview also confirms the founding space: "having seen it grow from a tiny, scrappy startup with **six of us in the basement of Mother**…" — this locates the very earliest POKE at Mother London's offices (Mother is at 10 Redchurch Street, Shoreditch). They later moved to their own Shoreditch space close to The Albion on Boundary Street.

### Early Culture & Approach

Nik Roope (LBBOnline, 2012):
> "We wanted to focus back on really strong, cut through ideas and recognise that when you're on the internet you're competing against everything else and therefore you just have to be bloody good, or bloody interesting, or bloody funny or bloody scary."

Iain Tait (Boards Magazine, March 2010 — sourced via W+K London blog):
> He gave a talk at PSFK London three years prior (so ~2007) called "**Ten Reasons Why Digital Is Better Than Advertising**." Key themes: entrepreneurialism within digital, the structures in place in digital agencies to produce *things* (vs. advertising agencies that produce advertising), and passion.

The POKE blog/wire on pokelondon.com (archived at Wayback Machine, 2007 snapshot) had a characteristic informal voice. The agency organised events including a "ClubPub" event (July 2004), a fete event (August 2004/2005), participated in Designersblock (September 2004), and had a playful side project called "**Pokia**" (a Nokia phone/toy, September 2004).

The Wayback Machine 2007 snapshot of pokelondon.com shows the site featured a dropdown of "features" that included both campaign showcases and a "Wire" blog. The portfolio featured projects including: DK Travel Guides, Topshop/Kate Moss, Penguin Malice Box Quest, Top Gear, Kate Moss, Zopa.

## 5. STRUCTURE & SIZE

Tom Hostler (Creative Review, Jan 2025): "having seen it grow from a **tiny, scrappy startup with six of us in the basement of Mother** through to being quite **a creative force**."

The "six of us" at founding matches: Iain Tait, Nicolas Roope, Simon Waterfall, Nick Farnhill, Peter Beech, Tom Hostler.

Nik Roope described Oven Digital growing to 75 people, and mentioned POKE as a more deliberate, smaller-scale operation. No specific headcount for POKE during 2001–2009 found in sources.

### Ownership Structure

At time of 2013 Publicis acquisition:
- Mother Holdings Ltd: **50.01%** (majority stake)
- Four founding shareholders: minority collective (Roope, Farnhill, Beech, Hostler — Tait and Waterfall had already departed)

This confirms Mother held majority from early on (likely an investor from inception, given the "basement of Mother" origin story).

### POKE New York

At time of Publicis acquisition (Sep 2013): "Prior to the acquisition, POKE New York completed a **management buyout** of the company, relaunching as the new, independent digital innovation company **Makeable**." POKE NY founders: Michael Kantrow and Tom Ajello. (POKE NY was founded sometime between 2001–2009; exact year not confirmed in research.)

### Nik Roope's Side Ventures (not POKE products)

Roope (LBBOnline 2012) confirms **Hulger** and **Plumen** are personal ventures outside POKE: "There's Hulger, which are consumer products. We used to make a lot of telephones and now we make lots of designer light bulbs under the brand Plumen."

## 6. DEPARTURES & TIMELINE

- **Simon Waterfall** left POKE: **September 2009** with no job lined up. (Marketing Week, Feb 2010: "Co-founder Simon Waterfall left the agency in September 2009 with no job to go to.") He founded Fray Ltd in 2009 and later went to Intel (2012–2014) and Airbnb Samara Lab.

- **Iain Tait** announced departure: **3 February 2010**; joined W+K Portland as digital ECD, **late March 2010**. He had been commuting from Brighton and cited wanting to be a better father. Nicolas Roope confirmed: "Iain has had a tremendous amount of change in his life in the last year." Emma Pueyo (from DoubleYou Barcelona, joined 2009) took on creative director role alongside Roope. Source: Marketing Week, 3 Feb 2010 (confirmed on newmediaage.co.uk originally).

## 9. KEY GAPS / UNCONFIRMED ITEMS

- **"New Media Age Most Admired Digital Agency"** — specific years not confirmed beyond 2008 (NMA + MW). The "twice in past seven years" claim from Design in Britain implies at least two wins in 2003–2010 window. Specific year(s) besides 2008 unknown from this research.
- **POKE office address** — phone number (+44 20 7 324 5134) suggests an EC area number consistent with Shoreditch/Clerkenwell. Likely on or near Redchurch Street or Boundary Street, Shoreditch. Exact street address not confirmed.
- **POKE staff size at peak** — not found in sources reviewed. Oven Digital grew to 75; POKE was described as much smaller and more deliberately so.
- **"Poke are 1000 days" feature** — wire item from 2004 (visible in 2007 archive). POKE's 1000th day would have been approximately late September 2003 (if founded Jan 2001) or late November 2003 (if founded March 2001). This could help narrow the exact founding month.
- **NMA "Most Admired Digital Agency" vs "Agency of the Year"** — it's unclear if these were the same award or two different NMA awards. The Waterfall list says "2008 Agency of the Year NMA + MW." The phrase "Most Admired" is what Iain Tait may have remembered/used separately.
- **BIMA wins beyond 2004** — the 2005 wire item "Doubles at BIMAs 2005" (30 November 2005) suggests two BIMA wins in 2005, but specific categories not confirmed in this research.
- **The Malice Box ARG** — visible in 2007 POKE portfolio dropdown. A project for the ARG for Martin Langfield's novel. Extent/awards of this project not confirmed.
