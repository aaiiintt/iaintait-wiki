## 5. Iain's Specific Role

- **Co-founder of POKE London** — confirmed in TechCrunch, The Drum, Creative Bloq
- The Creative Bloq interview is the most detailed source on Iain's perspective on BakerTweet
- BakerTweet credited as "brainchild of Nik Roope" (per Creative Bloq interview with Iain), with Andrew Zolty as developer
- Iain's role: as POKE co-founder and creative director, he was the public face discussing BakerTweet's creative philosophy and its place in POKE's R&D culture
- Iain framed it as emblematic of POKE's approach: "I think R&D should be the responsibility of everyone"

## 6. Cultural Legacy & Influence

### MoMA Exhibition
- Included in MoMA's "Talk to Me" exhibition (2011) — one of the most significant design exhibitions of the decade, focused on communication between people and objects
- BakerTweet was installed at MoMA's Cafe 2 during the exhibition

### Pioneer of IoT / Physical Computing in Advertising
- Repeatedly cited as an early example of "Internet of Things" before the term was mainstream
- Wired framed it as innovative physical computing
- Adafruit coverage placed it in the maker/Arduino community
- Campaign cited it as a key example of "how to drive digital marketing forward in 2010"

### Published in "The Idea Writers" (2010)
- Academic/industry book by Teressa Iezzi (AdAge editor) references BakerTweet as an exemplar of new-era copywriting/creative

### Spin-off Interest
- A zoo inquired about "KeeperTweet" (per Iain in Creative Bloq)
- Nicolas Roope discussed productisation potential (per LBB)
