## Summary of Findings

A short film/ad titled **"An Awesome World"** was definitively made for Google Chrome and launched on **8 October 2012** (US), accompanied by a Chrome Blog post ("It's an awesome world") and featured on Hulu and TV in addition to YouTube. The film tells the story of Dallas Clayton, who self-published *An Awesome Book!* for his son and became a viral self-publishing success story.

**The director credit could NOT be definitively confirmed from publicly accessible web sources.** The Ad Age Creativity listing for the film exists at `https://adage.com/creativity/work/awesome-world/29468/` and shows a "Credits" section header, but the credits content is behind a paywall and did not render in the scrape. No other public source found explicitly names the director. David Gelb's involvement is plausible and consistent with the timeline (see below), but should be marked as **unconfirmed / per Iain's recollection only** until a primary source is found.

## 2. Director: Was It David Gelb?

**Status: Plausible but unconfirmed by any public web source found in this research session.**

Iain Tait (ECD at Google Creative Lab at the time) recalls the director went on to direct Chef's Table (Netflix). Chef's Table was created/directed by **David Gelb**. David Gelb also directed *Jiro Dreams of Sushi* (shot 2010, US theatrical release March 9, 2012; Netflix streaming from August 28, 2012).

**Timeline consistency check:**
- *Jiro Dreams of Sushi* theatrical release: March 2012
- *Jiro* on Netflix streaming: August 2012
- Google Chrome "An Awesome World" film launch: October 2012
- Chef's Table Season 1: 2015

David Gelb would have been a compelling choice for Google Creative Lab in mid-2012: *Jiro Dreams of Sushi* had just launched on Netflix and was generating significant cultural buzz. A short documentary about a self-publishing father's dream would be in his wheelhouse (intimate, character-driven, beautifully shot documentary filmmaking).

**No public web source found that explicitly names David Gelb as the director of this film.** The Ad Age credits page (the most likely source for this confirmation) is paywalled. The YouTube video has been removed. No press coverage found that names the director.

**Recommendation:** This should be recorded as "Director: David Gelb (per Iain Tait's recollection — unconfirmed by web sources)" and flagged for verification via Ad Age Creativity subscriber access or direct confirmation from David Gelb / Google Creative Lab alumni.

## 6. Dallas Clayton / Google Chrome Collaboration: Context

- Dallas Clayton self-published *An Awesome Book!* in 2009 after publisher rejections
- Published it online for free; went viral; sold 50,000 copies in 18 months via social media
- Amazon published his second book *An Awesome Book of Thanks!* under AmazonEncore imprint
- HarperCollins signed him for a 3-book deal (books republished April 2012)
- Google Creative Lab selected his story for a "web film" as part of the broader Chrome "It Gets Better"-style human stories campaign
- The film featured Dallas and his son
- Book was made available in the Chrome Web Store in 17 languages
- The film aired on Hulu and TV (in addition to YouTube)
- 186,000+ YouTube views in fewer than 3 months after launch
