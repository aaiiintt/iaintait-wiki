## Part 1: Google Chrome "For Everyone" Campaign (2013)

### Campaign Name and Tagline

- **Campaign tagline:** "For Everyone"
- This is the confirmed tagline, not "The Web is What You Make of It" (which was an earlier BBH campaign). The 2013 campaign produced with 72andSunny/Google Creative Lab used "For Everyone" as the tagline, confirmed by iSpot.tv records on the TV spots.
- Adweek's 2013 Agency of the Year article describes Google Chrome as "For Everyone," even including "nettlesome cartoon characters like Stewie from Family Guy."

### Agency Situation — BBH vs 72andSunny

**Important clarification:** There appear to have been overlapping agency relationships:

- **Ad Age (February 22, 2013):** "72andSunny Picks Up Google Chrome Assignment" — article by Shareen Pathak confirmed 72andSunny was working on a campaign for Chrome as of February 2013.
- **iSpot.tv records for the March 2013 TV spots:** Both spots ("Family Guy" and "Lost Directions", both published March 22, 2013) list the creative agency as **BBH**, not 72andSunny. Media agency: PHD USA. Digital agency: Essence.
- **Adweek Agency of the Year 2013:** Names Google Chrome "For Everyone" as one of 72andSunny's key 2013 clients and campaigns (subscription-only full text, not accessible).

**Possible interpretation:** The TV spots may have been executed by BBH (which had the Chrome account previously), while 72andSunny may have worked on different components or took over from BBH during the campaign period. Alternatively, iSpot.tv's agency attribution may be incomplete or incorrect, attributing to the prior agency of record rather than the new relationship. Further research needed to clarify.

The Ad Age article (Feb 2013) says: "72andSunny can now add Google Chrome to its assignment list. The agency is currently working on a campaign for the browser, according to executives familiar with the matter." This clearly predates the March 22, 2013 launch of the "For Everyone" TV spots.

**Google Creative Lab role:** Iain Tait was ECD at Google Creative Lab during this period. Google Creative Lab would have functioned as internal creative director/client overseeing external agencies. In this model, GCL sets creative strategy and brief, partner agencies execute. This is consistent with the known GCL operating model from this era.

## Part 2: "An Awesome Book" — Chrome Web Store App (October 2012)

### Overview

A Chrome Web Store interactive app adaptation of the children's book *An Awesome Book!* by Dallas Clayton. Dallas Clayton is described as "a dad who wrote a book for his son about dreaming big, fantastic dreams." The book was made available on the Chrome Web Store with interactive/animated web adaptations.

### Google Chrome Blog Post

- **Title:** "It's an awesome world"
- **Published:** Monday, October 8, 2012
- **URL:** https://chrome.googleblog.com/2012/10/its-awesome-world.html
- **Blog:** Chrome Blog — The latest news from the Google Chrome team
- **Note:** The blog post itself is live but the body content was not fully captured in this research pass (the blog appears to strip body text from the page). The October 2012 blog archive confirms the post exists.

**Blog description (from search result snippet):** "It was about a dad, named Dallas Clayton, and a little book he wrote for his son about dreaming big, fantastic dreams – called An Awesome Book!" [Confirmed from search snippets]

### Dallas Clayton

- Dallas Clayton is an author and artist, described as self-publishing *An Awesome Book!* originally for his son
- The book was available in 17 languages by the time of the Chrome launch
- It is downloadable from the Chrome Web Store
- An Aussie business site (Anthill Online, November 2, 2012) covered the story as: "How Google Chrome turned a dream into a self publishing success story"
  - URL: https://anthillonline.com/how-google-chrome-turned-a-dream-into-a-self-publishing-success-story-video/

### Format

- Interactive Chrome Web Store app
- Web-based, animated/interactive version of the book
- Available in 17 languages
- Described by Anthill as a demonstration of Chrome's capabilities for storytelling
- Produced by Google Creative Lab (Iain Tait era), though specific GCL credits not found in open sources

### Related Coverage

- LA Weekly (2019 archive) mentions Dallas Clayton's subsequent books: *An Awesome Book of Thanks!*, *An Awesome Book of Love!*
- Amazon Prime ran a series in 2015 based on his book *Lily the Unicorn*

### Iain Tait's Specific Role

Not confirmed in publicly available sources. Given his role as ECD at Google Creative Lab from approximately 2012, he would have been the creative lead overseeing this type of Chrome Web Store creative project. The "An Awesome Book" app sits in the same category as other GCL-produced Chrome experiences (Chrome Experiments, Chrome Web Lab, etc.).

## Context Notes

This campaign sits within a broader Google Chrome marketing narrative:
- Chrome advertising began with "Parisian Love" (Super Bowl 2010, BBH/Google Creative Lab)
- Followed by "Dear Sophie" (2011) and other BBH-produced emotional storytelling spots
- 72andSunny's engagement (announced Feb 2013) marks a handover/expansion of the agency roster
- The "For Everyone" humor-based approach (featuring pop culture characters like Stewie) was a tonal shift from the earlier emotional storytelling
- 72andSunny went on to win Adweek's US Agency of the Year for 2013, with Chrome cited as a highlight client

Google Creative Lab under Iain Tait (ECD from approximately 2012) would have been the strategic and creative oversight layer above all external agency work.
