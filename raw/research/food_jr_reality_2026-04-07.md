## 1. Project Overview

**Full Official Name:** JR Reality
**Subtitle / Tagline:** "The largest participatory art project in the world" / "The first Augmented Reality community network"
**Type:** Mobile AR app experience (iOS and Android)
**Platform:** Built on Niantic's Lightship Visual Positioning System (VPS) / Lightship AR platform

JR Reality is a location-based augmented reality community art platform that extends JR's existing _Inside Out Project_ (launched 2013) into the digital world. Anyone with a smartphone can attach a black-and-white portrait photograph and a voice recording to a geo-anchored floating "living mural" at a physical location in their city. Other users can then discover and interact with those stories in AR, or remotely view them inside the app.

The project is described by its makers as "a worldwide canvas that connects people and ideas."

## 3. How It Works — The Mechanic

- **App-based experience:** Free to download on iOS and Android from US app stores (as of public launch, US only; international expansion planned).
- **App store links:** iOS: https://apps.apple.com/us/app/jr-reality/id6445825357 | Android: https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.superblue.jrreality
- **App home:** Available exclusively through the Superblue app, later as a standalone JR Reality app.
- **Core mechanic:**
  1. User creates an account in the app.
  2. User goes to a geo-mapped mural location (shown on an in-app map).
  3. User takes a black-and-white portrait (selfie style) and records a voice message — together called a "story."
  4. User optionally tags their story with "Themes."
  5. User places their story in AR, anchored to that physical location.
  6. Other users can visit the same location and see all the AR portraits floating there (the "living mural"), and tap individual portraits to hear the voice messages.
- **Remote viewing:** Users not physically present can browse murals and see/hear stories remotely in the app.
- **"My Stories" feature:** Users can create a story at home, and it saves locally until they visit a mural location to post it.
- **Niantic Lightship VPS:** The core technology that makes the AR content persistent and geographically anchored. Niantic's 3D digital twin of cities ensures "a shared frame of reference, even on a grand scale" — all devices see the same AR content in the same physical location.
- **Technical note:** JR Reality was described as "the very first app using Niantic's breakthrough AR Developer Kit 2.0 technology" (per Inside Out Project FAQ).

## 6. Collaborators & Their Roles

### JR (Jean René)
- French street artist and photographer, anonymous public figure
- **Role:** Artist, creative originator, the artwork and concept are entirely his. JR Reality is his digital extension of the _Inside Out Project_.
- His studio is credited as **JR Studio** in all project materials.
- JR's quote: *"Have you ever passed somebody on the street and wondered what their story is? Or looked up through a window and wondered who lives there?"*
- JR personally appeared at Elizabeth Street Garden in Soho, NYC on 16 May 2023 (public launch day) to explore AR murals with the public.

### Superblue
- **What they are:** Superblue is a ground-breaking enterprise dedicated to supporting artists in realising their most ambitious visions and engaging audiences with experiential art. Co-founded by Marc Glimcher (Pace Gallery) and Mollie Dent-Brocklehurst. Launched 2021 with a 50,000 sq ft space in Miami's Allapattah neighbourhood. The venture works with leading experiential artists including James Turrell, Es Devlin, teamLab, DRIFT, and JR.
- **Role on JR Reality:** Lead producing partner and platform host. Superblue commissioned, produced, and drove the project. The app was the "Superblue app" in its initial phase. Superblue provided the commercial and institutional framework.
- **Key quote (Mollie Dent-Brocklehurst, Co-Founder & CEO):** *"We are thrilled to launch JR Reality as the first of many projects that exemplify our ongoing commitment to developing augmented and virtual reality opportunities for artists to expand their art with cities as their canvases."*
- App support email: appsupport@superblue.com

### Niantic
- **What they are:** The developer of Pokémon GO and creators of the Lightship AR platform — described as building "augmented reality technology that powers the real-world metaverse."
- **Role on JR Reality:** AR technology platform provider. Niantic's team of in-house developers worked directly on the project alongside Nexus Studios. Their Lightship Visual Positioning System (VPS) is the underlying spatial technology that makes AR content persistent and geo-anchored. Niantic provided the "3D digital twin" of cities.
- **Key quote (Dan Morris, Director of Developer Relations, Niantic):** *"At Niantic, we're driven to create immersive AR experiences that inspire movement, exploration, and shared experiences. We're excited to partner with artworld disruptors Superblue and JR to take shared art experiences to the next level."*
- JR Reality was featured as the hero project at Niantic CEO John Hanke's keynote at the Lightship Summit (DevCon) in May 2022.

### Nexus Studios
- **What they are:** London-based immersive experience and AR studio with a track record including Google AR, U2 AR, Gorillaz x Google, BBC Civilisations XR, and numerous Niantic projects.
- **Role on JR Reality:** Experience design and technical build partner. Nexus Studios designed and built both the beta (2022) and the public launch version (2023) of the app. Their own portfolio features two separate case study pages for JR Reality — one for the 2022 beta and one for the 2023 public launch.
- **Key quote (from Nexus Studios case study):** *"We worked closely with the renowned artist himself, alongside his studio, JR Studio, Superblue, and Niantic's team of in-house developers to leverage the power of the Lightship VPS, and produce a beta experience."*
- **Key quote (from public launch case study):** *"JR Reality is the artistic evolution of a stunning analog idea into the digital world. Through Niantic's VPS, we're able to create these amazing persistent, shared artistic experiences across the world, which has never been possible before. This is just the start — there's a lot more bloom to come from this flower!"*
- Instagram: @nexusstories

### FOOD (Arts & Technology)
- **What they are:** Creative company co-founded by Iain Tait. Based in London. Described on their own website (food.xyz) as working across "Arts & Technology."
- **Role on JR Reality:** Confirmed as a collaborator by FOOD's own website, which lists "JR Reality. Global AR Art Project with JR Studio / Niantic / Nexus / Art Practice" as a project in their portfolio (from the food.xyz search result snippet: *"JR Reality. Global AR Art Project with JR Studio / Niantic / Nexus / Art Practice"*).
- **Specific role:** Not explicitly described in any public press coverage — FOOD is not mentioned in the launch press releases, which credits only Superblue, JR/JR Studio, and Niantic. However, FOOD's website credits them alongside Art Practice (the production arm of Time Based Arts). FOOD's likely role was **creative direction / strategic creative concept and development** — bridging between JR's artistic vision and the technical execution.
- Note: The Instagram @nexusstories (Nexus Studios) is tagged in the official Superblue Instagram post, but FOOD is not named in social posts. FOOD's role was likely behind-the-scenes / pre-production phase.

### Time Based Arts / Art Practice
- **What they are:** Time Based Arts is an award-winning VFX and Colour Grading studio (London), working across advertising, film, episodic, and music videos. Art Practice is named as their collaborative studio partner (Instagram bio: *"In collaboration with @artpractice.studio"*). Art Practice is also confirmed as a JR Reality collaborator by FOOD's website listing.
- **Role on JR Reality:** FOOD's website lists both "Nexus" and "Art Practice" as project partners. Art Practice is likely the production / post / finishing partner for the project, possibly handling video content, visual identity, or launch materials.
- Note: Time Based Arts / Art Practice are not credited in any public press coverage about JR Reality. Their role appears to have been in the production execution rather than public-facing.

## 14. Relationship to Inside Out Project

JR Reality is explicitly positioned as the digital extension of JR's _Inside Out Project_ (est. 2013). Inside Out invites communities to create and display large-scale black-and-white portrait installations in public spaces — "actions" that have engaged 450,000+ participants across 141 countries. JR Reality translates this into an always-on, smartphone-accessible AR version where the portraits are virtual and voice-enabled.
