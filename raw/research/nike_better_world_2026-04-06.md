## Dimension 4 — Metrics

No independently published traffic/conversion metrics in public sources. Brand/sustainability platform — not a direct-response campaign. Cultural reach confirmed via: Most-discussed website of 2011 (Smashing); FWA Site of the Day; Webby winner; Google Trends parallax data rises sharply from 2011.

## Dimension 5 — Iain's Role

- Role: Global Interactive Executive Creative Director, W+K Portland
- Joined W+K 2010; site launched January 2011
- Executive oversight of all W+K interactive output — same relationship as Old Spice Responses
- No source explicitly names him as a direct maker; role is as ECD above the team
- Direct makers: Seth Weisfeld (ICD), Ryan Bolls (producer), Ian Coyle, Duane King
- OregonLive (Dec 2011) confirms Tait's remit: "overseen the award-winning interactive" campaigns: https://www.oregonlive.com/playbooks-profits/2011/12/wiedenkennedy_names_4_new_part.html

## Dimension 6 — Cultural Legacy

- Cited by multiple sources as the first mainstream parallax scrolling website
- GWS Media, One Page Love, TNW/FWA all credit it as the catalyst for the parallax trend
- FWA juror called it a "developer coming-of-age" moment
- Smashing Magazine (2011): "stands as an example of what a great idea and some clever design and development techniques can produce"
- Ian Coyle and Duane King went on to co-found King/Coyle (2010–2015) and collaborate with Huge
- David Jones (CEO Havas) cited it in "Who Cares Wins" as exemplar of Social Business Idea
