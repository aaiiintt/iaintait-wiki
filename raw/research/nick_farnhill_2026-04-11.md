### Education
- Computer science and information technology (with a year in Holland)
- 1994: Nijmegen University, Netherlands — discovered the internet
- Source: The Drum 2018

### How he came to digital
- Original intention: run a hotel. Not allowed to go to catering college.
- Studied CS instead. Met Simon Waterfall, David 'Gravy' Streek and Gary Lockton at early Deepend.
- "In 1994 at Nijmegen University I discovered the internet and was hooked."
- Source: The Drum 2018

### POKE founding
- Founded 2001 after Deepend collapsed in the dotcom bust
- Co-founders confirmed: Nick Farnhill, Nik Roope, Tom Hostler, Peter Beech, Iain Tait
  - Note: The Drum 2018 quotes Nick saying "my co-founders Nik Roope and Tom Hostler" — suggesting these were his closest working relationships within the founding group
  - More About Advertising 2022 notes "joint venture with Mother" who "put a bit of money in"
- Note on Simon Waterfall: appeared in index.md as a co-founder — confirmed by The Drum (he's mentioned at Deepend, alongside Nick) but not named as POKE co-founder in the sources above. Needs clarification.
- Sold to Publicis in 2014 for £10m (confirmed: More About Advertising 2022; The Drum 2014 interview says "we've just sold the agency recently")

### FOOD founding
- Co-founded with Iain Tait and Richard Turley
- Registered November 2021; publicly announced August 2022
- "It's bittersweet to leave the company that's been such a part of me for so long, but there was always going to be a right moment to move on and start something fresh again." — Nick Farnhill, Adweek June 2021
- Source: Adweek 2021, More About Advertising 2022

### The Lovie Awards
- Founded 2010 by Nick Farnhill as "joint venture with the Webby Awards"
- European sister of the Webby Awards
- Nick continues as Co-Chair
- 2015: awarded Lifetime Achievement Award for contribution to digital advertising industry
- Source: Apprentice Nation, LinkedIn

### Tech City Apprenticeship
- Co-founded and launched 2013
- Source: Apprentice Nation

### Hackney House
- Co-created and led 2014 and 2015
- Source: Apprentice Nation

## Synthesised Notes

Nick Farnhill is primarily the **business/CEO half** of the POKE and FOOD partnerships with Iain. While Iain and Nik Roope were the creative leads, Nick ran the agency. His trajectory — from Deepend partner to 20-year POKE/Publicis CEO — is essentially one of continuity and loyalty to the same founding creative vision, followed by a clean break and return to independent work with Iain.

The most important edge for the knowledge graph is: Nick → POKE → Publicis acquisition → FOOD. The Lovie Awards is a significant independent strand that creates edges to the European internet/digital industry ecosystem separately from the agency work.
