- `raspbian-addons` — Modifications to stock Raspbian configuration

## Dimension 5: Iain's Role (ECD, Champion)

Iain Tait was ECD of Google Creative Lab NYC during this project. His role was:
- **Executive Creative Director** — the creative leader overseeing the team responsible for Coder
- **Active champion** — per Iain's own account, he was closely engaged with Jason Striegel's work and actively advocated for the project internally

The project aligned directly with Google Creative Lab's mandate to produce open, useful, and creatively-driven experiments at the intersection of technology and culture. Coder represented a departure from purely visual/campaign work — it was a functional software product with a clearly defined social purpose (democratising web education).

As ECD, Iain would have:
1. Provided the creative leadership framework enabling Striegel's technical vision to be built and shipped
2. Made the internal case for the resource investment
3. Helped position it for public release and press strategy
4. Ensured it aligned with Google's broader education and open-source identity

This is a pattern consistent with how the best creative lab output gets made: a single technical creative talent (Striegel) with a strong concept, enabled and championed by a leader (Tait) who clears the path, shapes the narrative, and decides to ship.

## Dimension 6: Cultural Legacy (Maker Culture, Education, Open Source)

Google Coder landed at the peak of the Raspberry Pi cultural moment (2012–2015) when makers, educators, and parents were actively searching for approachable tools. The Pi had sold millions of units but web-based creative coding on it was technically demanding. Coder removed that barrier entirely.

**Cultural context:**
- The Raspberry Pi Foundation was simultaneously gifting 15,000 devices to UK schools (with Google as a funder)
- The "maker movement" and STEM education were mainstream conversations in both tech and mainstream press
- Codecademy had launched in 2012 and was growing rapidly; Khan Academy's CS curriculum was new
- Coder was positioned alongside both of these — making it a hardware companion to browser-based coding education

**What made it culturally significant:**
1. **Zero-command-line setup** — the 10-minute install was a real differentiator; previous Pi web server setups required terminal expertise
2. **Runs in Chrome** — meeting learners where they already were
3. **Classroom scalability** — the `findcoder-appengine` experimental component suggests Google Creative Lab was thinking about multi-device classroom scenarios
4. **Open source ethos** — released on GitHub from day one, explicitly welcoming forks and community contributions
5. **Google's name** — lending credibility and discoverability to an educational experiment that might otherwise have been niche

**Lasting footprint:**
- Archived (not deleted) in August 2025 — 12 years after launch, the repo was still considered worth preserving
- 269 community forks indicate continued developer interest beyond the original team
- The "Coder Projects" companion site (https://googlecreativelab.github.io/coder-projects/) was built as a separate resource, suggesting investment in curriculum/content alongside the tool itself

## Summary

Google Coder (2013) was an open-source project from Google Creative Lab NYC that turned a Raspberry Pi into a simple personal web server and browser-based HTML/CSS/JavaScript development environment. It was built primarily by Jason Striegel (Creative Technologist) and Jeff Baxter (Designer), with Iain Tait serving as ECD and active champion.

**Key verified facts:**
- Launched September 12, 2013 — simultaneous coverage in TechCrunch, Engadget, Fast Company, with The Verge following September 14
- TechCrunch explicitly names Jason Striegel and Jeff Baxter as the builders
- GitHub: 2,400 stars, 269 forks, 194 watchers, 83 commits, Apache-2.0 license
- Archived (not deleted) on GitHub on August 18, 2025 — after a 12-year lifespan
- No formal advertising/creative awards found — the project was never entered into award circuits in the traditional sense
- Official Google YouTube launch video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wH24YwdayFg
- Docs site: https://googlecreativelab.github.io/coder/
- GitHub: https://github.com/googlecreativelab/coder
