## SECTION 2: ORIGIN STORY (Confirmed)

### Where he grew up
> "I grew up in a perfectly nice but entirely non-descript village in the middle of England."
— Creative Review Beginnings, 2019

**USER CONFIRMED (2026-04-11):** Born in Edinburgh, Scotland. Grew up in Barton under Needwood, near Burton on Trent, middle of England. Both facts accurate — born in Edinburgh, raised in Staffordshire. The Refinery29 "Scot" reference is therefore accurate (birthplace), and the Creative Review "non-descript village" is also accurate (upbringing). *(user testimony, 2026-04-11)*

### At school
> "I was a pretty decent student who wasn't entirely shit at maths and science."
— Creative Review Beginnings, 2019

> "I was okay at school. I always had some combination of 'could do better' or 'try harder' sprinkled in my reports."
— Refinery29, 2013

Parents bought him a computer when he was 10. Played 1980s video games obsessively — learned about failure and iteration.
> "Playing 1980s video games was very different back then. You died again, and again, and again. Every time, I had to go back to the start and try a slightly different tactic. I learned much about failing, over and over. It's a really valuable skill when you're trying to 'sell' creative work."
— Refinery29, 2013

### University
- Enrolled to study **engineering** (careers advisor's flowchart + liked Lego/computers)
- University: **University of Edinburgh** (LinkedIn: 1991–1995)
- **USER CONFIRMED (2026-04-11):** "University of Edinburgh, psychology BSc, and my side was in information systems" *(user testimony, 2026-04-11)*
- Degree: **BSc Psychology** with specialism in **Information Systems**
- His own account (Creative Review) confirms the engineering → transfer story:
  > "My only way out was to take an outside course and transfer onto that after a year. I struggled through a year of 9am maths lectures..."
  > "My professor ended up being a specialist in Human Computer Interaction (which sat in the Psychology dept). He got me early access to the Internet/WWW, and encouraged me to study Information Systems and Technology and Society."

**Synthesis:** Started as engineering student at University of Edinburgh ~1991, transferred after one year into BSc Psychology with Information Systems specialism, graduating ~1995. *(user testimony + AAF bio + Creative Review — triple-confirmed)*

### Discovering the web
- Professor of Human Computer Interaction gave him early access to the internet/WWW
- Remembers a specific lecture about "which Hollywood robots would get along best in the real world"
- At university, connected to people via computers and love of house music, swapping mixtapes online via email lists (Refinery29)
> "At the end of my degree I had no idea what I was going to end up doing. But I wanted to work 'in the Internet'."
— Creative Review Beginnings, 2019

### Early Edinburgh period (pre-London)
