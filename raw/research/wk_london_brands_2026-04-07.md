## 1. Honda "Dream Makers" (July 2017)

### Overview
A 90-second film created for Honda's Channel 4 film sponsorship, paying tribute to the Dream Makers and showcasing the craft and innovation that goes into filmmaking. The film takes viewers through each stage of the filmmaking process — scripting, storyboards, CGI, grading — centred on four characters and four Honda models (new Civic, Civic Type R, CR-V, Africa Twin motorcycle), with each scene depicting a different film genre. The process is an analogy for the passion behind great films and Honda vehicles.

### Where It Ran
- **Channel 4 sponsorship:** "Film on 4" — shorter idents ran alongside all films on Channel 4, More4, Film4 and E4 for 12 months
- **Online and cinema:** Longer 90" film also deployed online and in cinemas beyond the sponsorship idents
- **Launch date:** 18 July 2017

### Awards

## 2. Bud Light UK Launch (March 2017) + "Keep It Bud Light" TV Campaign (October 2017)

### Overview
The first-ever Bud Light campaign in the UK market. The brand had attempted a UK launch ~16 years prior without success. This represented AB InBev's biggest-ever UK brand campaign. Two distinct phases:

**Phase 1 — UK Launch OOH (March 2017)**
- National out-of-home and digital campaign launched 13 March 2017
- Tagline: "Keep It Bud Light" (the overarching brand platform)
- Strategy: Highlight Bud Light's "light-hearted personality" with youthful, uncomplicated, playful messages nodding to American heritage
- Appeared in iconic locations: London IMAX, Birmingham Media Eyes
- Followed three months of experiential and influencer "tease" activity
- Multi-channel strategy predicted to reach 90% of UK adults 14+ times
- Included digital spend across Facebook, Snapchat, YouTube, Instagram; bus wraps, PR, social
- Bud Light also launched on draught in Wetherspoon pubs UK-wide

**Phase 2 — "Keep It Bud Light" TV Spot (October 2017)**
- Third national campaign since UK launch (per The Stable)
- 30-second TV ad airing across national TV channels and cinemas, October–November 2017
- Parody lifestyle ad concept: ad opens like a typical lifestyle ad ("Super cool girl, aspirational friends… Lifestyle ad with Bud Light at the end")
- Director: Ali Ali (Egyptian director, his first-ever UK ad; ex-ECD and founder of agency Elephant; one of the world's ten most awarded directors per Gunn Report)
- Production company: Sonny London

## 3. Tyrrells Crisps: "Absurdly Good" — First-Ever TV Campaign (September 2017)

### Overview
Tyrrells' first-ever TV advertising campaign, launched 11 September 2017. Campaign budget: £2.5 million. Supported by video on demand, outdoor and social media. Introduced a new tagline: *"It's absurd what we do before Tyrrells gets to you."*

The campaign brings to life the iconic black-and-white characters from the Tyrrells packaging, giving them a distinctly English eccentric voice, provided by actor and impressionist **Peter Serafinowicz** (known for The Tick, Guardians of the Galaxy, Shaun of the Dead).

Two executions:
1. **"Very Important Potatoes"** — 20" TVC, plays on absurd lengths the Herefordshire crisp maker goes to make its hand-cooked crisps
2. **"Fabulously Curly"** — 20" social film

Both are centred around Tyrrells Court Farm in Herefordshire, humorously bringing to life the way the brand selects the finest potatoes. The campaign ran until end of November 2017.

## 4. National Trust: #EveryoneNeedsNature (September 2020)

### Overview
A fundraising and awareness campaign launched 7 September 2020, capitalising on the COVID lockdown period when connection to green spaces had become paramount to people's wellbeing. The campaign was originally planned and paid for in March 2020 but had to be put on hold during lockdown.

Context: The National Trust furloughed 80% of its staff and had to pause/cancel conservation projects due to severe financial losses from the pandemic. The campaign launched to raise funds and awareness for the charity's 125th anniversary conservation ambitions: planting 20 million trees and creating green corridors for nature.

**Key campaign message:** "Everyone Needs Nature" / "#EveryoneNeedsNature"

**Donation structure:**
- £5 buys and plants a tree
- £10 helps maintain coastal footpaths
- £15 helps with wildflower meadow creation

**Six TVCs** leading the campaign — each focused on nature and wellbeing. The ads show a sunrise, a beach, a child skipping stones, prompting viewers to visit nationaltrust.org.uk to "find more calm."

**Run:** Six weeks from 7 September 2020 across ITV, Channel 4 and Sky; billboards around the country; national newspapers and magazines.

**Supporting data (YouGov poll, June 2020):**
- 38% of adults said spending time in nature was the moment they looked forward to most during lockdown
- 33% said their interest in nature had increased since lockdown
- 68% agreed/strongly agreed that noticing nature made them feel happy during lockdown

## 5. Sainsbury's: "Helping Everyone Eat Better" (May 2021)

### Overview
Launched 26 May 2021. Sainsbury's first new brand platform in a decade — retiring the long-running "Live Well for Less" motto (used since approximately 2011) and replacing it with "Helping Everyone Eat Better." Subplatform: "One Plate at a Time."

A future-facing brand repositioning reflecting Sainsbury's belief that delicious food — healthy, innovative and good for the planet — should be accessible to everyone. Tied to Sainsbury's commitment to reach Net Zero by 2040 and its position as Principal Supermarket Partner for COP26 (Glasgow, November 2021).

**Hero executions:** 60" TVC. The film opens with what appear to be aerial shots of Earth's landscapes, which the camera reveals are macro close-ups of healthy food — a match-cut visual device showing produce transformed into landscapes. Stephen Fry delivers a rallying cry: *"If you ever find yourself worrying about our planet or your health, start with dinner… Grab your plate and start making a difference."*

**Context:** Government research found only 27% of UK adults aged 19–64 eat their five-a-day. Sainsbury's provided a suite of recipes to help customers eat more fruit and veg.

**Run:** TV ad 26 May to 12 July 2021. Supported across print, social, OOH, digital, VOD and radio. Food photography, people/icon photography, and in-house production also formed part of the campaign.

**Metrics:** The Sainsbury's YouTube channel shows the "Helping Everyone Eat Better" film accumulated 3.2 million views.
