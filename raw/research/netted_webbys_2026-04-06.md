## Summary of Key Findings

POKE London (specifically Nicolas Roope and Iain Tait) provided the **naming and branding/design work** for Netted by the Webbys, a daily email newsletter property launched by The Webby Awards. The engagement is directly confirmed in a primary source quote from David-Michel Davies, Executive Director of The Webby Awards, published on MarketingSherpa in September 2011.

---

## 1. What Was Netted?

**Full name:** Netted by the Webbys (also known simply as "Netted")
**URL:** https://www.netted.net (still live as of 2026)
**Parent organisation:** The Webby Awards / Webby Media Group / Recognition Media
**Tagline:** "Better Living Through the Internet"

Netted was a free daily email newsletter published by The Webby Awards team. Each edition recommended a single website, app, or service that the editors deemed worthwhile, indispensable, or life-changing. The editorial mission was described as helping readers "use the Internet to master modern life in ways both big and small."

At peak the newsletter reached 50,000+ subscribers. Arianna Huffington was quoted in promotional materials: "Nobody knows the web better than The Webbys team. If Netted says an app is killer or a site is a must-visit, it is."

**Official press release date:** November 4, 2009

The Webby Awards issued a formal press release titled "The Webby Awards Launch Daily Newsletter Uncovering Internet's Most Brilliantly Useful Things" dated **New York, NY (November 4, 2009)**.

## 3. POKE London's Involvement — PRIMARY SOURCE

The critical primary source is a September 22, 2011 article in **MarketingSherpa Blog** titled "Naming and Branding: How marketing pros chose names for their own companies":

> "Work with an agency and people you trust on the naming assignment. I took our **branding/design assignment** to **Nicolas Roope and Iain Tait at Poke London** (Iain's now at Wieden + Kennedy) because I knew that when I asked them to make something both beautiful and Internet-y with a great name, they'd understand what that meant for our organization."
>
> — David-Michel Davies, Executive Director, The Webby Awards

**Source URL:** https://sherpablog.marketingsherpa.com/marketing/naming-branding-companies/

This is a direct first-person statement from the client (David-Michel Davies) confirming:
- POKE London was engaged for the **branding and design** work on Netted
- Both **Nicolas Roope** (POKE co-founder/ECD) and **Iain Tait** (POKE ECD/co-founder) were the contacts named
- By the time of writing (September 2011), Iain had already moved to Wieden + Kennedy Portland
- The name "Netted by the Webbys" itself emerged from this creative/naming process

### Nature of the Work
Davies explains the naming challenge in detail — they needed to integrate the Webby Awards brand without causing confusion. Three concerns drove the brief:
1. People might think covered sites were Webby Award winners
2. The newsletter's scope was narrower than what the Webby Awards honours
3. Associating the Webby brand with a daily publication could muddle the awards' positioning

The name "Netted by the Webbys" was the outcome that elegantly resolved all three concerns. Davies attributes the creative approach ("both beautiful and Internet-y with a great name") to the POKE team.

## 4. Additional Connections: Claire Graves / POKE → Webby Awards

A further POKE–Webby connection is documented via Claire Graves, who is described in the Lovie Awards speaker bio as:

> "Prior to joining The Webbys, Claire was a Client Lead at one of the UK's most celebrated interactive advertising agencies, **Poke London**. There she managed the strategic design and build of brand Websites and communications for The Guardian, The BBC, Skype, Orange, Topshop, and the children's charity Barnardos. Her work at Poke includes several **Webby Award winning projects**."

Claire Graves went on to become Executive Director of The Webby Awards. This suggests POKE had a substantial ongoing relationship with the Webby Awards organisation beyond just the Netted naming/branding project.

**Source URL:** https://www.lovieawards.com/about/tour/speakers-2/

## 5. Status of Netted in 2026

The site https://www.netted.net remains live and active as of April 2026, with articles published through early 2025. The property still promotes the Webby Awards and its editorial content focuses on apps, tools, and digital products. Content has shifted from daily emails to periodic feature articles.
