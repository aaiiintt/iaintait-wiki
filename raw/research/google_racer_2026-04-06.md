## Dimension 4: Metrics & Cultural Impact

**From the Cannes Lions 2014 submission (primary source):**
- 3.2 million website visitors
- 70% of users coming from mobile or tablet
- Coverage was "universally positive"
- Focus areas in coverage: innovative use of technology within the mobile browser, use of WebSockets and the Web Audio API

**From Active Theory technical case study:**
- 8 weeks of development leading up to Google I/O 2013 launch
- Technical challenge: multi-player game without native app install
- Had to sync car position within 10ms across devices
- Poll-based server time offset calculated as median of multiple round trips for accuracy

**Cultural impact — spin-offs:**
- "Racer S" — a separate follow-on experiment by HelloEnjoy (December 2013), listed on Experiments with Google: https://experiments.withgoogle.com/racer-s (described as "a WebGL demo optimised for mobile browsers")
- Community-made reverse-engineered servers still exist: github.com/Technohacker/recar and github.com/allancoding/racer — evidence of lasting developer interest

**Google I/O 2013 live demo:**
- Racer was demonstrated live on stage at Google I/O 2013 (confirmed by multiple sources including Cannes submission and The Verge coverage)

## Dimension 5: Iain's Role (ECD, patent inventor)

- **Role:** ECD (Executive Creative Director) at Google Creative Lab NYC
- **Patent:** US10195523B2 — "Multi-screen gaming using a portable computing device as a controller"
  - Inventors: Iain Tait, Stewart Smith, Jeffrey Paul Baxter
  - Filed: May 10, 2013 (priority date — same day as Google I/O 2013)
  - Granted: February 5, 2019
  - Assignee: Google LLC

**Key detail from Active Theory case study (web.dev):**
> "Armed with the concept, design and prototype from Google Creative Lab and sound from Plan8 we iterated on builds for 8 weeks leading up to the launch at I/O 2013."
- This confirms that Google Creative Lab (Iain's team) originated the concept, design, and prototype, while Active Theory built it out over 8 weeks.
