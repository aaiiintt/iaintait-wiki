### Campaign context: the #PlugLife phenomenon
The electrical plug character was unplanned as a viral element — it was one of several children in the background of the school play scene. The public latched onto the deadpan delivery of the actor. Harrison Wilmot subsequently received media coverage and was invited to switch on his hometown's Christmas lights, extending the campaign's PR reach significantly. This contextual arc is confirmed via the wklondon.com case study.

### Song licensing note
"You Get What You Give" by New Radicals (1998) is one of the most recognised songs from the late 1990s. The choice reflects Sainsbury's recurring strategy of using known but emotionally resonant music (cf. Nicholas the Sweep's use of an orchestral score by Chris White performed by the London Metropolitan Orchestra). No new information about licensing terms found.

### Michael Gracey career context
At time of the ad's production (autumn 2018), Michael Gracey had just directed *The Greatest Showman* (2017), the Hugh Jackman musical that became a long-tail box office phenomenon. This was a significant talent acquisition for a 2-minute retail Christmas ad — indicative of both W+K London's ambition and Sainsbury's budget commitment. Partizan is one of the foremost commercial production companies globally and Gracey was their director.
