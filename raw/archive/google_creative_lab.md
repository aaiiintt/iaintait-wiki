## Agency: Google Creative Lab NYC (2012-2014)

### Project: Roll It (Chrome Experiment)

- **Year**: 2013
- **Collaborators**: Legwork and Mode Set
- **Overview**: A web-based game that reimagines the classic arcade game Skee-Ball. Built as a Chrome Experiment.
- **Technical Context**:
  - Required syncing a smartphone to a computer browser using WebSockets.
  - The phone acted as a motion-sensitive remote (using the accelerometer) to "flick" a ball from the phone onto the desktop screen.
  - Desktop scene was rendered in WebGL and Canvas using Three.js for 3D physics.
- **Legacy**: Served as a major breakthrough showcasing what the mobile web could accomplish without needing native app downloads. Won recognition from Webby Awards and FWA.

### Project: Racer (Chrome Experiment)

- **Year**: 2013
- **Unveiled at**: Google I/O 2013
- **Collaborators**: Active Theory (Development), Giorgio Moroder (Soundtrack)
- **Overview**: A multi-player, multi-device "slot car" style racing game. Up to five people could connect their phones or tablets together, creating a shared, continuous race track that spanned across all physical screens.
- **Technical Context**: Utilized WebGL and web sockets for millisecond-perfect syncing across devices and form factors.
- **Awards/Legacy**: Cannes Gold Lion. Widely recognized as a major technical achievement in cross-device synchronization and open-web gaming.

### Project: Chromebook "For Everyone"

- **Year**: 2012-2013
- **Collaborators**: 72andSunny
- **Overview**: A massive launch campaign for the introductory $249/$349 Chromebook. The campaign aimed at repositioning the complex OS as a highly accessible device "for everyone".
- **Scale**: Included a massive Times Square takeover and multi-channel tactical "scrappy" ad deployments to simplify the messaging.
