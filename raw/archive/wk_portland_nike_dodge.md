## Project: Nike Better World

### Client: Nike

### Agency: Wieden+Kennedy Portland

- **Launched**: Early 2011
- **Overview**: A sustainability-focused platform for Nike, aiming to present impact stories cohesively on the web.
- **Significance**: Widely recognized in the web design industry as the *first mainstream, high-profile example of parallax scrolling used for a landing page*.
- **Technical/Design Achievement**: Demonstrated how moving background and foreground elements at different speeds while scrolling could create a 3D illusion of depth and a cinematic, immersive storytelling experience using HTML5 and extensive JavaScript/jQuery.
- **Legacy**: Prior to this site, parallax scrolling was primarily associated with 2D video games. The success and aesthetic impact of the *Nike Better World* site popularized this technique across modern web design, leading to its widespread adoption for product landing pages, portfolios, and brand experiences worldwide. While the URL `nikebetterworld.com` was eventually taken down, it remains a heavily cited case study in web design history.

## Project: Dodge Tent Event (Chimp Removal)

### Client: Dodge / Chrysler

### Agency: Wieden+Kennedy Portland

- **Launched**: 2010
- **Overview**: In the 2010 "Dodge Tent Event" advertising campaign, the initial commercial featured a real chimpanzee (named Suzy) driving a vehicle.
- **Agile / Crisis Response**: Following criticism from animal welfare organizations regarding the use of great apes in entertainment, Dodge rapidly altered the commercial.
- **Technical Execution**: They used CGI to remove the chimpanzee from the ad entirely and added a self-aware, humorous voiceover updating the script to refer to an "invisible monkey" instead.
- **Significance**: A quintessential early example of "real-time" or highly agile social response management, adapting live broadcast creative directly to social sentiment and turning a PR vulnerability into a celebrated meme. Dodge also subsequently issued a statement committing to not using great apes in their advertisements.
