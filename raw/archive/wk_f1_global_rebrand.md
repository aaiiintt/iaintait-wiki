## Agency: Wieden+Kennedy London

### Project: Formula 1 Global Rebrand ("Engineered Insanity")

- **Client**: Formula 1
- **Year**: November 2017 (Logo Reveal) / March 2018 (Campaign Launch)
- **Overview**: A complete visual identity and strategic overhaul designed to reposition Formula 1 as a global media and entertainment brand for a new era.
- **Design Leads**: W+K London, led by design director Richard Turley (known for Bloomberg Businessweek).
- **Strategic Foundation**: Grounded in an in-depth global brand health study conducted with research agency Flamingo to uncover what fans truly valued about the sport.
- **Launch Campaign ("Engineered Insanity")**: The rebrand's first major marketing push launched with a 60-second film focused on the visceral, elemental forces of high-octane racing. It famously utilized six real F1 superfans as the emotional core, exposing them to a literal wind-tunnel/force simulation.
- **Media Impact**: The logo reveal generated coverage across 1,500 media outlets globally within five days and massive social media engagement.
- **Out of Home**: Supported by extreme proximity media, hand-painted murals, and airport takeovers at key Grand Prix cities (Melbourne, etc).
- **Legacy**: Represented a historic shift in how a major, historically conservative global sports property visualized itself for a modern, digital-first audience.
