## Agency: Wieden+Kennedy London

### Project: Nothing Beats a Londoner

- **Client**: Nike
- **Year**: 2018
- **Overview**: An "ultra-local" campaign celebrating the diversity, grit, and competitive spirit of young people living in London.
- **Narrative Structure**: The film depicts various neighborhoods across London, following a "passing the baton" format where characters engage in humorous one-upmanship, bragging about their extreme training conditions, only to be topped by the next person.
- **Cast**: Features over 100 genuine London-based sports enthusiasts, from amateur athletes to celebrity cameos (like Skepta, Giggs, and Mo Farah).
- **Production**: Directed by the collective Megaforce and produced by Riff Raff Films. Creative direction by Mark Shanley and Paddy Treacy.
- **Impact/Legacy**: The campaign was lauded for its incredibly authentic representation of London youth culture and its innovative, hyper-localized marketing strategy. It is frequently cited as a benchmark for how global brands can successfully connect with a hyper-specific local community by deeply embracing its unique vernacular.
- **Awards**: Won numerous top-tier creative awards, including the Cannes Lions Grand Prix and Titanium Lion.
