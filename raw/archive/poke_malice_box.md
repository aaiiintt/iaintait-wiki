# Source: crackunit.com (Iain Tait's Blog)

Date: Jan 17, 2007
Title: Google Maps Adventure Game for Penguin
Author: iaintait

## Content

If you’re into adventure type games. Or just want to win some great prizes. Or are a real nerd and want to see how we’ve used Google Maps as part of our quest interface. Head over to MaliceBoxQuest.com, something Poke have put together for Penguin to promote the launch of a new mystic-thriller book called, strangely enough, The Malice Box.
Tom and Cookie, two of the other Poke folk who worked on the game have also written bits about it on their blogs. In fact I think Cookie sums it all up rather well:
> The book is a bit like the Da Vinci Code – a dude runs about the place, solving puzzles and saving the world…For the online game we created, you get to navigate the globe using Google maps. Basically you solve puzzles, guess locations of ‘Red Gold’ on the map and win prizes. Go check it out and play the game and at <www.maliceboxquest.com/>

Oh, and if you’ve been paying attention this is the game that I was trying to find a scriptwriter for ages ago! And thanks to some helpful blog readers we eventually found Tim Wright, who was great to work with. Hooray for blogs!

# Source: MoneySavingExpert (Forum)

Date: 28 January 2007

## Content

Somewhere in the world, the Malice Box awaits activation. Launching on January 15th, the Quest will send players on a 5-week interactive global scavenger hunt in search of seven sources of Red Gold, the mysterious energy force that fuels the Malice Box. Without Red Gold, the Malice Box cannot detonate.

Players complete seven trials in which they will crack clues, break codes, and answer puzzles while navigating to the most remote parts of the globe using The Quad - a slick GPS device linked to Google Maps. For each trial players complete successfully, they earn valuable points. These points can later be exchanged for chances to "dig" - Battleship style - for the Malice Box. Players closest to the location of the Malice Box WIN prizes!

The Quest will immerse players in the world of Robert Reckliss, unsuspecting hero of The Malice Box, an adventure thriller of conspiracy and quest that links back to the alchemy of Isaac Newton. The game stems directly from the novel's plot, whereby Robert needs to complete 7 trials in 7 days to find the 7 keys necessary to prevent detonation of the Malice Box, a terrorist weapon of unimaginable power.

The Malice Box Quest has been designed by BAFTA-award winning creative company Poke. Players can register from 4th January at <https://www.maliceboxquest.com>. The Quest begins on 15th January.
