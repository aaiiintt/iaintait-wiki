## Agency: POKE London

### Project: BakerTweet

- **Client**: Albion Cafe (Shoreditch, London)
- **Year**: ~2009
- **Overview**: An innovative physical-to-digital IoT (Internet of Things) device built to allow cafe bakers to easily notify customers (via Twitter) when fresh baked goods were coming out of the oven.
- **Form Factor**: A rugged, physical box designed to survive a harsh commercial kitchen environment. It featured a physical dial to select the item (e.g., bread, croissants) and a button to dispatch the tweet.
- **Technology**: Built using an open-source Arduino board (Arduino Duemilanove and Ethernet Shield).
- **Origin**: Poke's office was located directly across the street from the Albion Cafe; the prototype was initially built selfishly so the agency could know when specific treats were fresh.
- **Awards/Legacy**: Cannes Lion Silver. Represented a very early, highly publicized, and charming application of brand-utility IoT and physical computing.
