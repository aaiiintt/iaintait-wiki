# Global Rich List

**Live URL:** https://www.globalrichlist.com/ (still live as of 2026)

A self-initiated POKE London project that inverted the concept of the Sunday Times Rich List. Instead of showing how poor you are relative to the super-wealthy, it showed how rich you are relative to the rest of the world. Enter your annual income; the site reveals your rank among the world's population — and almost always shows that Western users are in the top few percent globally.

Won **One Show New York Gold**, **2004 Viral Awards**, and **D&AD Finalist**. Reached 120,000 unique visitors in its first week. Still live and cited over 20 years later.

---

## Origin Story

The concept was co-originated by **Iain Tait and Nik Roope** in 2002, born from a conversation about how the Sunday Times Rich List makes you feel poor — and the realisation that flipping the comparison globally would have the opposite effect. POKE developed and launched it independently after several charities declined to adopt it without significant changes. Care International then approached POKE, recognising the perfect alignment with their mission.

**Nik Roope (WIRED, September 2003):** *"The idea is to really make people think about how rich they are compared to the rest of the world. In the West, we tend to obsess about celebrities and the super-rich. This is a really simple way to turn that on its head."*

---

## The Experience

Enter your annual income → the site places you on a global wealth ranking, drawing on World Bank Development Research Group data. A slide indicator shows your position relative to the world's population. An option to donate to Care International is presented alongside.

**Example from WIRED (2003):** *"An annual household income of $42,200 — the US median in 2001 — is enough to land someone in the world's richest 1 percent."*

The mechanic is designed to produce a moment of genuine surprise and perspective shift — making the user feel unexpectedly wealthy, which creates an emotional opening for a charitable ask.

## Metrics

| Metric | Value | Source | Date |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unique visitors, first week | 120,000 | WIRED / Nik Roope | September 2003 |
| First-week donations | ~$1,700 | WIRED / Nik Roope | September 2003 |
| Total visits | 1.5 million | Third Sector magazine | October 2005 |
| Daily traffic | Up to 1,000 hits/day | Third Sector magazine | October 2005 |
| Total donations raised | Just over £8,000 | Third Sector magazine | October 2005 |

**Note on donations:** Roope acknowledged the donate button wasn't prominent — the primary goal was shifting perceptions of relative wealth, not maximising fundraising.

At launch the site instantly topped Daypop, Blogdex and Popdex — the blog popularity indices of the era — and was covered by the London Guardian and USA Today within the first week.

---

## Cultural Legacy

Global Rich List is one of POKE's most enduring works:

- **Still live in 2026** — over 20 years after launch
- **Redesigned by POKE in 2013** for its 10th anniversary, adding a mobile-friendly scrolling interface and live World Bank Data API integration
- Cited in academic work including legal scholarship (St. Louis University Law Journal, 2020), university theses, and Kumi Naidoo's talks at the University of Michigan
- Referenced by **Giving What We Can** (effective altruism organisation) as a standard resource
- Described in 2013 as *"a standard source for visualizing income inequality"* (Cool Hunting)
- Named by Nik Roope in 2012 (LBB Online) as a signature self-initiated POKE project
