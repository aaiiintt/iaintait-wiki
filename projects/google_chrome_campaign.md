# Google Chrome: For Everyone

## The Campaign

The "For Everyone" platform — originated by Google Creative Lab for the Chromebook launch in October 2012 — was extended in early 2013 to Google Chrome browser, with TV spots airing from March 2013.

The creative approach positioned Chrome as the browser for everyone: not a power tool for developers, but a universal, effortless way to use the web. The campaign leaned into humour and pop-culture accessibility.

**Agency situation:** Ad Age (February 22, 2013) confirmed 72andSunny picked up the Google Chrome account. However, iSpot.tv records for the confirmed TV spots credit **BBH** as creative agency (with PHD USA as media and Essence as digital). The most likely explanation is that BBH completed or shared the TV work while the 72andSunny transition was underway, or that the TV was in production before the account moved. This requires further verification.

## Key Executions

**"Family Guy"** (published March 22, 2013)
- Stewie Griffin repeatedly saying "Mom" while Lois ignores him
- Seth MacFarlane voiceover
- Message: Chrome lets you pick up where you left off across devices
- **7.7 million views in its first week** — topped the Ad Age / Visible Measures viral video chart

**"Lost Directions"** (published March 22, 2013)
- Dog chews printed map directions
- Song: "Rawhide" by Frankie Laine
- Message: why print directions at all?

CNET reported the full campaign comprised **four spots** (two further spots not yet identified).
