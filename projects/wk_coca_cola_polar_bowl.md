# Coke Polar Bowl

> *Referenced in "The Idea Writers" (Palgrave Macmillan) and a 2024 Ad Age retrospective on campaigns that "hacked" the Super Bowl.*

## The Concept

Two animated Coca-Cola polar bears — one a New York Giants fan, one a New England Patriots fan — sat on their "snowfa" in an Arctic cave and watched Super Bowl XLVI live, in real-time, at **CokePolarBowl.com**.

The bears reacted to every play, the Madonna halftime show, and every commercial — including rival ads (they'd fall asleep during dull ones). The audience watching online watched *with* the bears, turning the broadcast into a shared, animated second-screen experience.

Super Bowl XLVI drew **111.3 million TV viewers** — a Nielsen record at the time.

## Technical Execution

This was not real-time CGI rendering — it was a sophisticated **live puppeteering system**. From a **control room at MLB Advanced Media in New York**, animators drove the bears using **Xbox game controllers** and a proprietary PC-based triggering interface, assembling pre-rendered animation libraries in real-time to match the game's unfolding events.

**Framestore** (New York and London) built the CGI bears and the real-time puppeting technology. **Animal Logic** (Sydney) handled the broadcast TV spot animation. **MLB Advanced Media** provided the streaming infrastructure.

Copywriter Heather Ryder confirmed: *"my partner and I digitally 'puppeted' the Polar Bears on game day."*
