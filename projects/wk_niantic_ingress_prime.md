# Ingress Prime: ADA vs. JARVIS

**Agency:** [Wieden+Kennedy London](../agencies/wieden_and_kennedy_london.md)
**Client:** Niantic
**Year:** 2018 (Ingress Prime launched November 5, 2018)
**Iain's role:** Executive Creative Director

## The Campaign

Launch campaign for *Ingress Prime* — the rebuilt version of Niantic's original AR game, the foundational technology that preceded Pokémon GO. Rather than a conventional product launch, W+K London created a live propaganda war between two artificial intelligences.

The two AIs represented the game's opposing factions:
- **ADA** — The Enlightened
- **JARVIS** — The Resistance

Both AIs were real. They generated their own propaganda imagery, posted it on social media, engaged in live conversations with players, and evolved — in genuine, measurable ways — based on how the community interacted with them. The campaign was designed to run in ongoing chapters, deepening over time as the AIs developed.

> *"How do you launch a refresh of a well loved game, and end advertising agencies in one? We created an actual (not fake) artificial intelligence that could be trained by gamers to create propaganda that would recruit more players to join this popular AR gaming experience. The more you recruit, the better your team will do."*
>
> — **Darren Simpson**, Creative Director, W+K London

> *"A propaganda film for the internet age. In an era of fake news and hacking to sway the views of the people of the world, two factions will embark on a propaganda war utilising the opposing AI systems named JARVIS and ADA."*
>
> — **Chris Boyle**, Director, Private Island

## The AI Ecosystem (Tool of North America)

Tool of North America built a complete six-component AI system:

**1. Natural Language Generation (NLG)**
A neural network trained on curated faction-specific text to give each AI a distinct voice. Used to generate propaganda image headlines and drive chat conversations. Continuously retrained from live player interactions — so each AI's language evolved the longer the campaign ran.

**2. Chat Interactions**
Players could talk directly to ADA or JARVIS. Their responses fed back into NLG training, meaning the AIs learned from every conversation.

**3. Personality Graphs**
A real-time graph of each AI's personality traits, updated via gradient descent. During conversations, players were shown multiple-choice image questions ("which image best represents your faction?") — their answers shifted the personality graph in real time, so the community was literally shaping each AI's character.

**4. Propaganda Image Generator**
Ingested the personality graph + fresh NLG headlines + editorial configuration and assembled AI-generated propaganda visuals. These were posted on social media multiple times per week. Thousands of source images and background videos were pre-tagged to personality graph values; the closest matches were assembled automatically.

**5. Reinforcement System**
After each propaganda image posted, it was fed back to players as a voting question. The winning image's values trained a neural network that optimised future content — a genuine reinforcement learning loop driven entirely by the community.

**6. Campaign Controller**
A full admin tool for Niantic to schedule, script, moderate, publish, test, and curate the campaign — and to extend it across future chapters as the AIs evolved.

**Scale:** Millions reached; hundreds of thousands actively contributed to AI training.

## What Happened

Ingress Prime launched November 5, 2018. The campaign went live alongside it, with the AIs posting, chatting, and evolving on social media. The community engaged — though the hardcore Ingress playerbase, deeply invested in the game's lore, had high expectations of the AI characters.

The old Ingress client ("Ingress [Redacted]") was shut down on September 30, 2019, causing significant community contraction. The campaign's designed chapter structure — built to extend over time as the AIs developed — never ran its full course.

Campaign Magazine's 2019 School Reports cited *Ingress Prime* as standout work from W+K London: *"Outside the realm of traditional advertising, Niantic's launch campaign for augmented-reality game Ingress Prime featured two opposing AIs trained by gamers in the art of persuasion."*
