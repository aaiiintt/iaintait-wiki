# Very Public Art Exhibition

A physical art exhibition installed in Selfridges' famous Oxford Street shop windows, where members of the public could bid on British art via SMS — standing on the pavement, looking through the glass. Designed by POKE London in 2003, years before mobile commerce or m-ticketing were mainstream concepts.

Won **BIMA 2003** and **One Show New York Gold 2004**.

---

## The Experience

Selfridges' Oxford Street windows — some of the most-viewed retail display space in London — became an art gallery. Each work was displayed with an SMS shortcode; passers-by could bid simply by texting from the street.

The name captures the concept precisely: art that was literally, physically very public — visible to everyone on Oxford Street, with participation requiring only the phone in your pocket.

**Why this was ahead of its time (2003):** Mobile commerce barely existed. SMS was the dominant mobile interaction. Using it as a real-time bidding and transaction mechanism for art — in a street-facing context — anticipated both m-commerce and the participatory retail experiences that would become commonplace a decade later.
