# Orange Talking Point

A debate and discussion microsite for Orange UK that pioneered an interface mechanic pre-dating the mainstream tag cloud and social media participation patterns. Users could engage with popular debate topics not by typing comments (the standard web forum model of the time) but by clicking on weighted, visual representations of topics and positions — a "click to add your voice" interaction that surfaced public consensus at a glance.

Won the **Webby Award in the Telecommunications category at the 10th Annual Webby Awards (May 2006)** — specifically cited for *"Pioneering social Media for Brand Engagement."* One of two Webby Awards POKE won in 2006 (alongside the the Jamie Oliver Feed Me Better campaign).

---

## The Innovation

In 2005–2006, online public participation still meant:
- Long-form forum posts (registration required)
- Amazon-style reviews (effortful)
- Blog comments (growing but still text-heavy)

Twitter did not yet exist. Facebook was US-college-only. Del.icio.us had pioneered tag clouds as a visualisation tool (c.2003–2004), but no one had turned the tag cloud into an *interaction mechanic* — a way to vote, participate, and make your voice felt by clicking.

Talking Point's innovation was exactly this: a popularity-weighted visual display of debate topics, where clicking registered participation, and the visual weight of each item reflected collective opinion. It predated what we now call upvoting, reaction buttons, and emoji responses by several years.

**Webby citation:** *"Won for 'Pioneering social Media for Brand Engagement'"* — framed at the time as an early form of social media, which is accurate. This was brand-hosted public discourse before any of the platforms we now associate with that concept existed.

## POKE–Mother Relationship

POKE London operated as the "digital sister agency" to Mother, which held Orange's main advertising account. This institutional arrangement — confirmed in Campaign magazine's September 2006 digital survey — was the route through which POKE won the Talking Point brief. The relationship had been running for approximately five years by 2006. Campaign scored the POKE/Mother partnership 6/7 ("Excellent"), citing Talking Point as the primary evidence.
