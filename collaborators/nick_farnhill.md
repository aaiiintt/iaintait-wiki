# Nick Farnhill

## Overview

Nick Farnhill is Iain Tait's longest-running professional partner — the business/CEO half of a creative partnership that has spanned three ventures over 25 years. While Iain and Nik Roope led the creative work at POKE, Nick ran the agency. His career arc — Deepend partner to 20-year POKE/Publicis CEO to independent FOOD co-founder — is one of continuity and loyalty to the same founding creative vision, followed by a clean break and return to independent work with Iain.

## Background

Studied computer science and information technology, including a year in the Netherlands. Discovered the internet in 1994 at Nijmegen University. Original ambition was to run a hotel — was not allowed to go to catering college, studied CS instead. Met Simon Waterfall, David "Gravy" Streek, and Gary Lockton at early Deepend. *(source: The Drum 2018)*

## Deepend

Partner at Deepend, a pioneering digital agency that grew to 300 people worldwide before collapsing in the dotcom bust (~2001). *(source: Adweek 2021, The Drum 2018)*

## POKE London

Co-founded POKE in 2001, weeks after Deepend's collapse, alongside Iain Tait, Nik Roope, Tom Hostler, and Peter Beech. Mother provided early seed funding. Nick served as Managing Partner / CEO throughout the agency's independent years.

> "About three or four weeks later a small group of us started POKE, and with the help of Mother actually at the time who put a bit of money in, we launched POKE."
> — Nick Farnhill, YouTube/Source LF Career Advice, 2014

POKE was acquired by Publicis Groupe in September 2013 for £10m. Nick remained CEO through the transition and a series of promotions: Joint CEO of Publicis.Poke (2014), CEO of Publicis London (2016), CEO of Publicis Worldwide UK (2018), and finally CEO of the merged Publicis•Poke entity (2019) combining Publicis London, Poke, Arc, and August. *(source: The Drum 2018, More About Advertising 2022)*

He departed Publicis in June 2021 after 20 years in total.

> "It's bittersweet to leave the company that's been such a part of me for so long, but there was always going to be a right moment to move on and start something fresh again."
> — Nick Farnhill, Adweek, June 2021

## FOOD Arts & Technology

Co-founded FOOD with Iain Tait and [Richard Turley](richard_turley.md) in November 2021 (company registered); publicly announced August 2022. *(source: More About Advertising 2022, Creative Review 2023)*

## The Lovie Awards

Founded in 2010 as a joint venture with the Webby Awards — the European sister award. Nick continues as Co-Chair. In 2015, he received a Lifetime Achievement Award for his contribution to the digital advertising industry. *(source: Apprentice Nation, LinkedIn)*
