# Simon Waterfall

## Overview

Simon Waterfall co-founded POKE London in 2001. He came from **Deepend/DeepGroup**, where he was Founder & Creative Director (1995–2001) — a different lineage from the rest of the POKE founders who came via Oven Digital. His Deepend work included winning a **BAFTA Interactive Award for Cartoon Network** (Best Entertainment Website, 2000).

At POKE, Waterfall's defining project was the **Alexander McQueen website** — a relationship begun at POKE's founding (~2001) and running for eight years. The McQueen site won **BAFTA Best Design Website 2005** — his second BAFTA.

Waterfall left POKE in **September 2009** with no job lined up. He subsequently founded Fray Ltd, then worked at Intel (2012–2014) and Airbnb Samara Lab.

His personal CV at `waterfall.co.uk/cv/talks-awards` is a primary source for POKE awards confirmations.

## Distinctions

- **D&AD President 2008** — youngest ever and first digital president
- **Royal Designer for Industry (RDI)** — awarded September 2008 (UK's highest design honour)
- D&AD progression: Design Week Awards judge (2005) → D&AD Digital Foreman (2006) → D&AD Vice President (2007) → D&AD President (2008)
- Design in Britain (Design Museum, 2010): profiled alongside POKE's "voted number one agency twice in the past seven years"
