# Iain Tait

**Subject of this wiki.** Creative director, co-founder, and maker. 25 years working at the intersection of technology, culture, and creativity.

## Overview

Iain Tait has spent a quarter of a century making things that didn't quite exist before — and doing it with enough consistency that it stopped being luck a long time ago.

He co-founded POKE London in 2001, at a moment when "digital agency" was still a phrase that needed explaining. In the years that followed, POKE became one of the most original and influential interactive studios in Britain: making participatory art installations for Tate Modern, building one of the first viral social good campaigns in *Global Rich List*, and developing playful experiments in AR and connected hardware long before either of those things had mainstream names. The work didn't follow trends — it tended to arrive before them.

In 2010 he joined Wieden+Kennedy Portland as Executive Creative Director, inheriting accounts that demanded the highest standard and finding ways to push further. The *Old Spice Responses* campaign, which his team helped shape into a real-time social phenomenon, redefined what interactive advertising could be. *Coke Polar Bowl* used live puppeteering technology to turn the Super Bowl into a shared second-screen experience — a format that had never existed before and hasn't quite been replicated since. Even *Dodge Invisible Monkey*, a crisis turned on its head, became a case study in how to convert a PR problem into a cultural moment. He was named Partner at W+K in December 2011.

From Portland he moved to Google Creative Lab, where he led work including *Google Racer* and *Roll It* — multi-device browser experiences that used the then-novel idea of phones as controllers, making Chrome feel magical rather than functional. These were technical experiments that millions of people chose to play with. Two patents came out of this period.

In 2014 he returned to Wieden+Kennedy London as ECD, beginning what would be a seven-year run. The work here was consistently surprising in its range: an iconic city-scale celebration of London through Nike; a global rebrand that gave Formula 1 its visual identity back; deepfake technology used to honour the legacy of an artist, not exploit it. And then, starting with Niantic, a run of work that was genuinely unlike anything else in the industry. The first-ever above-the-line campaign for Pokémon GO used real-time game data and turned billboards into PokéStops. *Global Catch Challenge* sent influencers across Japan and inspired a global community to catch 3.36 billion Pokémon in a week. *Ingress Prime* deployed two living AI characters — propaganda-generating, community-trained, personality-evolving — in what is now recognised as a template for AI-driven brand storytelling that the industry is only beginning to catch up with. He left W+K in 2021.

FOOD, which he co-founded that same year, has continued in the same spirit. *JR Reality* — built on Niantic's ARdk 2.0 as the first app to use it — gave anyone with a phone the ability to permanently anchor a portrait and a voice message to a physical location, turning the world into a living mural. AI work with Google has explored territory that few agencies are positioned to even understand, let alone do well.

What runs through all of it is a particular quality of curiosity: genuinely interested in what technology can do, not just what it can be seen to do. The best of the work tends to arrive at a question — *what if the billboard was also a game?* *What if the chimp just wasn't there?* *What if two AIs went to war for your loyalty?* — and then answer it with something fun.

## Background

Born in Edinburgh, Scotland. Grew up in Barton under Needwood, near Burton on Trent — "a perfectly nice but entirely non-descript village in the middle of England." *(Creative Review 2019)*

Parents bought him a computer at age 10. Played 1980s video games obsessively — learned about failure and iteration:

> "Playing 1980s video games was very different back then. You died again, and again, and again. Every time, I had to go back to the start and try a slightly different tactic. I learned much about failing, over and over. It's a really valuable skill when you're trying to 'sell' creative work."
> — Refinery29, 2013

## Education

**University of Edinburgh** (1991–1995) — BSc Psychology with specialism in Information Systems.

Enrolled to study engineering (a careers advisor's flowchart + love of Lego and computers). Transferred after one year into Psychology via Human Computer Interaction. His HCI professor gave him early access to the internet/WWW and encouraged him to study Information Systems and Technology and Society. *(Creative Review 2019, user testimony)*

> "At the end of my degree I had no idea what I was going to end up doing. But I wanted to work 'in the Internet'."
> — Creative Review Beginnings, 2019

Connected to people at university via computers and a love of house music — swapping mixtapes online via email lists. *(Refinery29 2013)*

### Pre-POKE

**Edinburgh Festivals** (1995–1999): Designed early websites for the Edinburgh Science Festival, Edinburgh International Festival, the Book Festival, and the Puppet Festival. Freelance work running alongside his Syzygy role. *(Creative Review 2019, user testimony)*

**Syzygy** (Aug 1996–Aug 1999): Strategist at one of the earliest European digital agencies. Three-year formative period. *(LinkedIn)*

**First Tuesday** (Sep 1999–Sep 2000): Director of Product Development at the legendary London dotcom-era networking organisation. Building products to scale beyond physical meetups into recruiting, venue properties, and freelancing. Quit two weeks before the company sold — the startup referenced in his recurring "money isn't a reason to stay" philosophy. *(LinkedIn, user testimony)*

**Oven Digital** (Sep 2000–Sep 2001): Business development. Met [Nik Roope](nik_roope.md) here — the meeting that led directly to POKE. *(LinkedIn, Nik Roope profile)*

### POKE London (Dec 2001–Apr 2010)

Co-founded December 2001 with [Nick Farnhill](nick_farnhill.md), [Nik Roope](nik_roope.md), [Simon Waterfall](simon_waterfall.md), Tom Hostler, and Peter Beech. Mother provided early seed funding. Offices in London and New York. Clients included American Express, Orange, Yahoo!, Zopa, Mulberry, Turkcell.

### W+K Portland (Apr 2010–May 2012)

Global Interactive Executive Creative Director. Named Partner December 2011. Based in Portland with wife and twin toddlers.

> "Wieden + Kennedy global interactive ECD Iain Tait is among four execs at the agency who can now add partner to their titles."
> — Adweek, December 2011

### Google Creative Lab (May 2012–May 2014)

Executive Creative Director, New York. Two patents filed from work originated in this period (see Patents section). Presided over Cannes Lions Cyber Jury 2012 while still transitioning from W+K.

> "The reason I came to Google was the chance to do things that have the potential to touch everyone."
> — Refinery29, 2013

### W+K London (Sep 2014–Jul 2021)

Executive Creative Director alongside [Tony Davidson](tony_davidson.md). Recruited [Richard Turley](richard_turley.md) to lead the F1 rebrand work. Board of Directors at The One Club for Creativity (Jun 2015–Mar 2021).

### FOOD (Nov 2021–present)

Co-founded with [Nick Farnhill](nick_farnhill.md) and [Richard Turley](richard_turley.md). Company registered November 2021; publicly announced August 2022. Office in Islington, London (by a canal, co-lead tenants with Made by Many). Business model: ~50% consultancy, ~50% owned/co-owned IP. *(LBB 2023, More About Advertising 2022)*

## Patents

**US10195523B2** — *Multiplayer game for display across multiple devices* (Granted)
Filed 2016-08-02 | Granted 2019-02-05. Co-inventors: Stewart Smith, Jeffrey Paul Baxter. System/method for a virtual gamespace divided across multiple web browsers so the 2D landscape is viewable when devices are placed together. Assigned to Google LLC. Almost certainly the direct precursor to [Google Racer](../projects/google_racer.md). [Google Patents](https://patents.google.com/patent/US10195523B2)
