# Nik Roope

**Relationship to Iain Tait:** POKE co-founder; former colleague at Oven Digital

## Background

Nik Roope studied conceptual art and sculpture at art school in Liverpool before entering the digital world through his brother's involvement with **Antirom**, an early digital art group headed by the late Andy Cameron. He went on to work at CHBi (which became European Razorfish) and then **Oven Digital**, where he met Iain Tait. When Oven Digital collapsed in the dotcom bust, Nik, Iain, and four others (Peter Beech, Nick Farnhill, Simon Waterfall, Tom Hostler) founded POKE London.

## At POKE

As co-founder and ECD, Nik was the creative originator behind several of POKE's most distinctive projects:

- **[Baker Tweet](../projects/poke_baker_tweet.md):** Credited as the "brainchild" of the BakerTweet concept — the Arduino-powered Twitter box for Albion Cafe that was later exhibited at MoMA.
- **[Kate Moss for Topshop](../projects/poke_kate_moss_topshop.md)** — Co-founder / ECD, POKE London (2007). Iain's direction; Nik confirmed as co-founder on the account — specific role unconfirmed pending credits research.
- Oversaw work for clients including Orange, Mulberry, and Turkcell.
- Served as **Jury Chairman for the Lovie Awards** (European sister programme to the Webby Awards), heading up the establishment of the European IADAS wing.

## Outside POKE

Nik runs product businesses separately from agency work:

- **Hulger:** Consumer products company, originally making telephones, later pivoting to designer light bulbs under the brand **Plumen**.
- Strong advocate for agencies creating their own products as a form of R&D and creative expression.

## Key Quotes

On POKE's founding: *"We were suddenly back to nothing again... we put a curtain on our very rapid ascensions that actually gave us a fresh start and gave us an opportunity to do it right."* (LBB, 2012)

On BakerTweet productisation: *"Bakertweet was a very quick, happy thing. If you want to productise something, suddenly you are looking at a very different level of investment."* (LBB, 2012)
