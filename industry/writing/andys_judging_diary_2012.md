# Andy Awards Judging Diary

**Publication:** Adweek (AgencySpy)
**Date:** February 29, 2012
**Author:** Iain Tait
**Affiliation:** Wieden+Kennedy Portland

## Synopsis

An extended first-person account of the 2012 ANDY Awards judging session, held in Kauai, Hawaii. One of Iain's most candid pieces of published writing — a rare inside view of how an award jury actually debates creative work, written from inside the room.

The diary surfaces tensions that Iain would continue to explore publicly: what constitutes a genuine idea vs. execution; the ad industry's tendency to reward work only within its own frame of reference; and the peculiar dynamics of getting a large group of senior creatives to agree on anything.

## Key Quotes

On the ANDY format:
> "As an advertising award, the ANDYs are pretty unique. It's one set of judges that presides over work from the entire industry."

On the central debate:
> "The most fundamental of all the conversations... is around what constitutes an idea."

On the industry's self-referential blind spot:
> "I also have issues with the ad industry patting itself on the back for making digital products and services when we're only comparing ourselves to a limited set of the work that's out there."

## Context

Written during Iain's W+K Portland tenure, between the 2011 Cannes Titanium jury and the 2012 Cannes Cyber Jury Presidency. It's a companion piece to the broader critique he articulated in his Cannes 2012 jury philosophy — that the most important work was defined by what brands *do*, not what they *say*.
