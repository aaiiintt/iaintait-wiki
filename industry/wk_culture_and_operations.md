# Wieden+Kennedy Operations & Culture Initiatives

While serving as Executive Creative Director at [Wieden+Kennedy London](../agencies/wieden_and_kennedy_london.md), Iain Tait paired with Deputy MD Helen Andrews to launch massive operational experiments and talent initiatives designed to fundamentally alter how the agency operated.

## "Working Differently" Culture Experiment (2016)

To combat chronic industry burnout and push back against W+K's informal "Weekend+Kennedy" moniker, Iain and Helen initiated a highly publicized operational experiment called **"Working Differently"**.

**Key Implementations:**

- **Email Curfew:** A total ban on internal emails between 7 PM and 8 AM.
- **"Brain-Time" Meeting Restrictions:** All meetings were restricted to between 10 AM and 4 PM, protecting early mornings and late afternoons for deep, uninterrupted work.
- **Time-in-Lieu:** Strict enforcement of time-in-lieu for any mandatory weekend work.

This initiative resulted in a major thought-leadership moment for agency operations, proving that a top-tier creative powerhouse could institute strict work-life boundaries without compromising the quality of output, a topic they later keynoted at The Story Conference in 2017.

## "The Kennedys" Talent Incubator (2016)

Iain was also instrumental in championing and importing **The Kennedys** incubator program from W+K's Amsterdam office into the London agency.

Designed to deliberately circumvent traditional, homogenous ad-school pipelines, the London incubator hired 6 people described affectionately as "freaks and misfits"—individuals with diverse, non-traditional backgrounds and zero formal portfolios. They were brought in for a paid, five-month crash course, drastically diversifying the agency's creative perspective and output.
