# Under the Influence

**Event:** Under the Influence (organized by Iris Worldwide)
**Year:** 2008 (April 17)
**Speaker:** Iain Tait
**Affiliation:** [POKE London](../../agencies/poke_london.md)

## Synopsis

In April 2008, the creative agency Iris Worldwide organized a unique event called **"Under the Influence"** in Borough Market, London. The format was highly unconventional: 20 talks spread across 5 different pubs in the market, with each pub dedicated to a different creative theme. Iain was scheduled to speak at the end of the day in one of the pubs, focusing on "immersive experiences."

Iain provisionally entitled his talk: **“The Use of Magic in Countering Human Adaptive Tendencies”** to sound grand and pretentious. In reality, it was a witty, self-deprecating presentation about digital culture and the internet. To prepare for the theme, Iain famously confessed that he bought a magic kit just to see if he could pull off some tricks during the talk. 

## Pub Atmosphere & Heckling

Because the talks took place in pubs and attendees were drinking throughout the day, Iain was famously nervous about the hazard of pub-style heckling. However, digital pioneer George Nimeh reassured him that the ad industry's most notorious provocateur and rebel, George Parker (AdScam), was speaking at the exact same time in another pub called **“The Runaway Tongue”**, drawing the hecklers away. Other industry figures like Faris Yakob were in attendance to support.
