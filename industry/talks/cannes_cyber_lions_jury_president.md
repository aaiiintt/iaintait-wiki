# Cannes Lions Cyber Jury President — Grand Prix Speech (2012)

**Event:** 59th Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity
**Role:** Jury President, Cyber Lions
**Date:** June 20, 2012 (ceremony) / February 3, 2012 (announced)
**Affiliation (announced):** Global Interactive ECD, Wieden+Kennedy Portland
**Affiliation (ceremony):** ECD, Google Creative Lab, New York

## Synopsis

The most significant single jury leadership role in Iain's career. Named Jury President of the Cannes Cyber Lions in February 2012 — at the time the most prominent jury chair role in digital advertising worldwide. The Cyber Lions was the category where the entire industry looked to understand what "digital" meant at its highest level.

By a quirk of timing, Iain had joined Google Creative Lab in May 2012, meaning that when he took the stage in Cannes in June 2012 to announce the Grand Prix, he was technically Google's first prominent awards platform appearance. Fast Company's live coverage referred to him as "Google Creative Lab executive creative director Iain Tait" — giving Google's new creative ambitions a very public, credible introduction to the Cannes audience.

## The Grand Prix Winners

Iain's jury awarded the Cyber Grand Prix to two pieces — the first time in Cannes history two Grand Prix were awarded in the Cyber category:

1. **Nike+ FuelBand** — Nike's wearable fitness tracker and platform (agency: R/GA)
2. **Curators of Sweden** — Sweden's official Twitter account handed to ordinary Swedish citizens (agency: Volontaire)

Both winners embodied what Iain articulated as the jury's thesis: work defined by what brands *do* rather than what they *say*.

## Key Quotes

**On appointment (February 2012):**
> "I'm excited to guide a Cyber Jury that brings together the best talent from around the globe to recognise the greatest work in the world. There's so much change taking place so rapidly in our space that it's going to be tough to make sense of it all. But I'm confident we'll prove this category is where creativity is vibrant, thriving, and shaping the industry."

**Announcing the Grand Prix (June 20, 2012):**

On Curators of Sweden:
> "Very simple, very smart and very brave... that they handed their voice over to the people says so much about who they are."

On the dual Grand Prix philosophy:
> "Both pieces are about brands behaving a certain way. They're not about what they say and noisy messages, they're about what brands do and that's what we're excited by."
> "We just felt these two pieces represented the kind of work that people should be creating."

## Context & Significance

- Preceded by Cannes Cyber jury membership in 2009 and Titanium & Integrated jury in 2011 — the presidency was the culmination of three years of building Cannes credibility
- The dual Grand Prix decision was unusually bold — awarding two top prizes made a statement about the breadth of what "Cyber" could mean
- Nike+ FuelBand (hardware-as-platform) and Curators of Sweden (brand-as-enabler) together echoed themes Iain had been articulating since the 2007 PSFK "10 Reasons" talk
- The Contagious "Lion Taming" PDF (2018) later cited the jury president's 2012 Cyber Lions press conference as an industry reference point
