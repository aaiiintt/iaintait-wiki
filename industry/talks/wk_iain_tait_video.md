# W+K's Iain Tait

**Format:** Video interview / profile
**Year:** 2011 (September)
**Affiliation:** [Wieden+Kennedy Portland](../../agencies/wieden_and_kennedy_portland.md)

## Synopsis

An interview filmed at the **2011 IAB MIXX Conference & Expo** during Iain's tenure as Global Interactive Executive Creative Director at Wieden+Kennedy Portland. The conversation centers on digital storytelling, the evolution of brand relationships, and the shifting pace of consumer expectations.

## Key Themes

1. **Digital Storytelling & Emotion**: Iain argues that digital channels are fully capable of delivering deep, emotional experiences (making people laugh or cry) and that failures to do so are issues of creative content, not limitations of the digital medium.
2. **Human Time vs. Brand Time**: He emphasizes that brands need to shift away from slow-moving "campaign time" and start operating in "human interactive time," adapting to the rapid, real-time expectations of connected consumers.
3. **Building Creative Worlds**: Iain discusses how successful modern campaigns construct their own integrated media worlds and notes his desire to collaborate with publishers who understand how to weave these brand narratives seamlessly into their own platforms.
