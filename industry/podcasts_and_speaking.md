# Podcasts & Speaking

As a veteran creative executive, Iain Tait has been sought after for his perspectives on digital marketing, agency culture, sustainability in advertising, and the impact of AI on creative industries.

## Notable Podcast Appearances

* **[Fast Company Brand New World — "The Next Creative Revolution" (2025)](podcasts/fast_company_brand_new_world.md)**
  * *Context:* Season 1, Episode 3 of Fast Company’s Brand New World podcast, discussing the next creative revolution.
* **MediaSense: Campaign’s Year Ahead (2025)**
  * *Context:* Featured at their Breakfast Briefing (and podcast). Iain aggressively argued that using AI purely for operational efficiency and margin-protection is reductive. He positioned its true value as alleviating busywork from leaders, giving them the bandwidth to return to actively nurturing and growing human talent.
* **Europe In Synch Podcast (2024)**
  * *Episode:* Act In Synch 2024, Athens Special – The Brands/Authenticity Talk
  * *Context:* A panel discussion exploring modern marketing challenges, brand authenticity, and the responsibilities of brands intersecting with cultural issues.
* **[Creative Review: Creativity Sucks! — AI Discussion (2023)](podcasts/creative_review_creativity_sucks.md)**
  * *Context:* Discussing AI and creativity on Creative Review’s podcast.
* **Telltale Stories Podcast (2019)**
  * *Context:* Discussing the advertising industry’s role in the climate emergency, detailing how massive global brands can responsibly engage with sustainability crises.

## Keynotes & Panels

Iain has spoken at numerous premier creative and digital platforms globally, including:

* **["10 Reasons Why Digital is Better than Advertising" at PSFK (~2007)](talks/psfk_10_reasons_digital.md):** The landmark presentation that argued digital was fundamentally better than advertising — widely cited as the talk that launched Iain’s trajectory from digital specialist to creative leadership.
* **[High Scores at Playful London (~2008)](talks/playful_london_high_scores.md):** Talk at the Playful conference exploring play, games, and interactive experiences.
* **[Under the Influence (~2008)](talks/under_the_influence.md):** Presentation from the POKE era.
* **[Radiohead Marketing Presentation (~2008)](talks/radiohead_marketing.md):** Analysis of Radiohead’s digital distribution experiments.
* **"Delete It All (Live)" at ADCE Creativity Festival (2023):** Representing FOOD, Iain delivered a highly provocative, performative keynote on the anxieties of Generative AI. After demonstrating how AI generated tens of thousands of images, apps, and 3D characters, he deleted over 60,000 images *live on stage*, forcing a severe reckoning regarding the true value of creativity when production becomes practically infinite.
* **["A Crowd of Terrible Content" at It’s Nice That’s ‘Here London’ (2015)](talks/here_london_2015.md):** A heavily quoted keynote where Iain pushed back against the industry’s hype-overload regarding new platforms (like Periscope/Meerkat). He argued that to digital natives, the internet is no longer a novelty, urging brands to stop fetishizing tech formats and return to authentic storytelling. He famously opened by claiming his career was "mostly one of cheating and blagging."
* **[D&AD: "I Wish I’d Done That" (2013)](talks/dandad_i_wish_id_done_that.md):** Film featuring Dan Wieden, Iain Tait, and Jessica Walsh.
* **[OFFSET Dublin (2013)](talks/offset_2013.md):** Conference appearance during Google Creative Lab tenure.
* **The Story Conference (2017):** Alongside Helen Andrews, Iain delivered a transparent, operational keynote detailing the results of the "Working Differently" culture experiment at W+K London, demonstrating how to run a top-tier creative agency without destroying its people.
* **Cannes Lions International Festival of Creativity:** Regular appearances, including high-profile "Big Tent" sessions (such as his 2012 session alongside Arianna Huffington representing Google Creative Lab).
* **99U / Adobe:** Presentations geared toward fostering creative culture and demystifying technology for creatives.
