**Founded:** 2001
**Acquired:** September 2013 by Publicis Groupe

## Overview

POKE London was an independent digital creative agency founded in 2001 out of the wreckage of the dotcom bust. Most of the founding team came from crashed dotcom-era agencies: Iain Tait and Nik Roope from **Oven Digital** (which grew to 75 people in London before collapsing — *"I had to fire everybody, including myself, all within 18 months"*); Nick Farnhill, Tom Hostler, Peter Beech and Simon Waterfall,  from **Deepend/DeepGroup**

POKE started with six people **in the basement of Mother London** (St John St). Mother held a **50.01% majority stake** from inception — an investment relationship that lasted until the Publicis acquisition in September 2013. The agency later moved with Mother to their Shoreditch space on Boundary Street, across the road from The Albion bakery.

Nik Roope on founding philosophy:
> *"We wanted to focus back on really strong, cut through ideas and recognise that when you're on the internet you're competing against everything else and therefore you just have to be bloody good, or bloody interesting, or bloody funny or bloody scary."*

Tom Hostler (Creative Review, 2025):
> *"Having seen it grow from a tiny, scrappy startup with six of us in the basement of Mother through to being quite a creative force."*

## Defining Works (per Boards Magazine, 2010)

> *"Oasis (Rubberduckzilla), Orange (Unlimited), and Nokia (Room 10101)"* — explicitly named as POKE's three defining works in a March 2010 Boards Magazine interview with Iain Tait (published via the W+K London blog at the time of his departure).

## Culture

POKE operated from the conviction that digital work had to compete with everything else on the internet — not just advertising. The agency built things that predated mainstream categories: social media for brands (Orange Talking Point, 2005), AR (Rubberduckzilla, 2009), IoT (Baker Tweet, 2009), ARGs (Malice Box, 2007), generative web art (Orange Unlimited, 2007).

POKE had a close working relationship with **Mother London** — not just as an investor but as a creative partner (Oasis and Orange accounts shared between the agencies for TV vs. digital). Iain Tait described POKE as "Mother's digital sister agency" in the context of Orange Talking Point.

The agency also maintained a playful side-project culture: **Pokia** (a Nokia phone toy shown at Designersblock 2004), **TeaBuddy** (2005), **"Poke Redesigns London"** (self-initiated, 2006).

## Structure & Ownership

- **Mother London** held 50.01% from founding
- Four founding shareholders held the remainder
- **POKE New York** existed as a separate entity; prior to the 2013 acquisition, POKE NY completed a management buyout and relaunched as **Makeable** (founders: Michael Kantrow and Tom Ajello)
- **September 2013:** Publicis Groupe acquired POKE London from Mother Holdings Ltd and the four founding shareholders
